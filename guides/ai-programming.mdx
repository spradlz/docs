#### ЁЯПа Buyer Phase 1 тАФ the full play (LOCKED, Zach 2026-07-23)

**Entry (unified):** ANY property request тАФ tour, property inquiry, contact, general тАФ from **FUB or HouseLoop, any
door тАФ converges to ONE first-touch flow.** (Kills today's dozen near-identical per-door workflows.)

**Two standing laws on the acknowledge тАФ every time:** (1) **always name the platform** they came through ("through
ZillowтАж"); (2) **always the agent's first + last name** on a first touch ("This is Ana Spradling with The Spradling
Group"), never just the first name.

**The sequence:**

1. **Acknowledge** (as the assigned agent) тАФ two forms:
   - **New lead** (standard first form): *"Hey Sarah, this is Ana Spradling with The Spradling Group. Got your request
     through Zillow to see 123 Main Street tomorrow at 11:00 AM. Does that time still work for you?"*
   - **Existing lead** (drop the full intro, bring context): *"Hey Sarah, Ana again! Just got your request from Zillow
     to see 123 Main Street tomorrow at 11:00 AM. Does that time still work for you?"* тАФ same closing question both ways.
2. **On "yes" тАФ buy time, and bridge straight into the questions** (we keep texting, we don't sign off):
   *"Perfect, let me check with the sellers real quick to make sure it works on their end. While I do thatтАж"*
3. **Behind the scenes тАФ always Myli** texts the **showing partner** (from the lead routing rule; today = Trish):
   *"Hey Trish, it's Myli тАФ got a buyer wanting to see 123 Main at 5. Told them I'm checking your availability."* тЖТ
   Trish replies (works / or a new time) тЖТ Myli: *"Perfect, I'll arrange it with the buyer and let you know once confirmed."*
4. **Simultaneously тАФ the casual qualification gates**, in order (no deep pre-qual, no pitch):
   *тАжare there any other homes in the area you might be interested in looking at?* тЖТ *Is this your first time getting
   out to see homes in person, or have you been at it a bit?* тЖТ **other agents comes NEXT, bridging off that answer:**
   *"Oh, that's awesome! Have you mainly been looking at open houses, or have you looked at any homes with an agent?"*
   (never let them corner you тАФ you're asking **how** they've shopped, not "do you have an agent") тЖТ *What's got you
   looking right now? Outgrowing your place, relocating, first home?* тЖТ **if another agent тЖТ** the representation gate:
   **"Gotcha тАФ have you signed any type of buyer agreement with them, or are you keeping your options open?"** (Zach-locked
   line). Signed/committed тЖТ courteous exit + `has_agent`. Options open / nothing signed тЖТ proceed, reassure:
   *"Perfect, just want to make sure we're not stepping on anybody's toes."*
5. **Close the loop** (no agent + gates clear): *"Just heard back тАФ 5 o'clock works! Here's what happens next: my
   showing partner Trish will meet you at the house tomorrow at 5:30. One quick housekeeping thing тАФ there's a single
   showing document that outlines how we're compensated if you decide to write an offer. No commitment to keep working
   with us, and the seller always covers our commission so it's nothing out of pocket тАФ it's just a form our KC broker
   has every agent use for showings. What's the best email to send a copy to? We'll just need it signed a couple hours
   before you meet Trish."*
6. **Send the showing agreement** (`send_document`) + its text: *"Hey Sarah! Here's that one-page showing agreement
   [link]. Sign whenever's easy for you, just need it back a couple hours before the showing. Any questions, I'm right here!"*
7. **Google Calendar invite** (from the assigned agent; buyer + partner), formatted:
   > Sarah and Trish, you're all set for 123 Main Street tomorrow at 5:30! So you've got each other's info:
   > Sarah Miller тАФ (816) 555-1234
   > Trish Redenbaugh тАФ (913) 555-9876
   > Enjoy the showing! Excited to hear how it goes. ЁЯПб
8. **Myli texts the showing partner:** *"Hey Trish тАФ got you set up, sent a calendar invite for [buyer]. Details:*
   **Property** ┬╖ **Date/time** ┬╖ **Buyer name** ┬╖ **Buyer phone** ┬╖ **Note:** *first-time buyer, just started looking,
   searching in [area]."*
9. тЖТ **The watching game begins** тАФ buyer confirmed, partner confirmed тЖТ **watch for the showing agreement to be
   signed** (Phase 1's real exit gate).

**The laws this phase locks:**

- **Myli is the universal assistant / secretary.** Even when an **AI clone is the talker** to the buyer, **Myli
  always does the orchestration** (partner coordination, sending the agreement, the calendar invite, the partner
  text). Buyer-facing messages go out as the **assigned agent**; Myli works behind the scenes. (Clone-calls-Myli vs
  just-tool-calls: keep it simple тАФ they're tool calls Myli owns.)
- **Unified first-touch** тАФ every property-request door fires the same flow.
- **New tool: `orchestrate_showing_partner`** тАФ contact + arrange the schedule with the routing-rule showing partner.
- **The showing-agreement gate** тАФ a single-page compensation disclosure (NOT the full exclusive buyer agreement)
  satisfies NAR at the *showing* level, signed a couple hours before the tour.

**The six columns (locked):** ЁЯОп confirmed calendared showing + agreement out ┬╖ ЁЯз░ `text_lead` ┬╖ `lookup_property` ┬╖
`orchestrate_showing_partner` (NEW) ┬╖ `send_document` ┬╖ `create_calendar_invite` ┬╖ `update_contact` (email) ┬╖
`update_stage` ┬╖ ЁЯФТ gate = showing agreement signed (couple hours pre-show) ┬╖ ЁЯС╗/ЁЯОБ/тЭдя╕П folded into the sequence
(fast, warm, human; the value IS handling the logistics for them).

#### ЁЯФБ Existing lead тАФ the returning buyer (the document escalation ladder)

Phase 1 **absorbs representation.** An existing lead runs the SAME 9-step flow with two differences: we **recall the
relationship** (continuity) instead of re-introducing, and we **escalate the document** тАФ the whole point is *we don't
re-paper them every single tour.*

First move: **look up what they've already signed** (`lookup_signed_agreements` тЖТ `{ showing_agreement?, buyer_agency? }`).
That drives the ladder:

| State on file | Action | Framing |
|---|---|---|
| Nothing signed (or brand new) | Send the **single showing agreement** | Runs the new-lead close (steps 5тАУ6) |
| Showing agreement on file **+ touring multiple homes** | **Pivot to the 6-month buyer's agency agreement** | *"one more, and it's the last one you'll sign"* |
| Buyer's agency on file | **No document** | Straight to "you're all set with Trish" |

- **Recall (continuity):** *"Good to hear from you again, Sarah! Last time you were looking around Waldo in the mid-4s тАФ
  still zeroed in there, or has the search shifted?"* тЖТ same seller-check + Trish setup.
- **The pivot (Zach-locked):** *"You're all set with Trish for 5:30! Last time you signed our single showing agreement.
  This time, since we're out looking at multiple homes, there's just one more document, and this'll be the last one
  you'll need to sign. It's a standing agreement for six months that lets us show you homes without signing something
  new every time. Same terms as before тАФ the seller covers our commission so it's nothing out of pocket, and you can
  cancel at any time."*
- **Already covered:** *"You're all set with Trish for 5:30 тАФ no paperwork this time, you're already covered!"*
- **Exit gate flips:** when we pivot, watch for the **6-month buyer's agency** signature, not the showing doc.

#### тП▒ When they go quiet mid-flow тАФ the low-texter fallback

Not everyone's a texter. After *"does 11 still work?"* three things happen: **they ghost** (тЖТ Ghosting Recovery),
**they answer the tour but skip the questions**, or **they go dark.** The rule: **Trish gets coordinated behind the
scenes no matter what** тАФ silence never stalls the showing. We keep the thread warm on a clock, then **collapse into
the document** when the timer runs out.

- **T+0** тАФ "yes, 11 works" тЖТ buy-time line + first question.
- **+30 min, no answer** тЖТ one warm nudge that re-asks the **NEXT** gate (not the same one): *"Still waiting to hear
  back from the sellers, should know any minute! While we wait, is this your first time getting out to see homes in
  person, or have you been at it a bit?"*
- **+60 min, still no answer** тЖТ stop asking, collapse: *"Hey Sarah, just heard back from the sellers, we're all good
  for 5:30! Quick check first: have you signed a showing or buyer agreement with any other agent? If not, there's just
  a single-page showing agreement that lets us show you this home and outlines how we're compensated, at no cost to you,
  the seller covers it. I'm going to text it over now, and as soon as it's signed you're confirmed!"*

The **signature becomes the gate** тАФ we fold the representation check into the confirmation instead of chasing answers.

#### ЁЯОУ Objection handling (the negotiate framing)

Answered from the knowledge base, calm and plain-English, never defensive. **Through-line: it protects you, it's
required, it costs you nothing.** Money is ALWAYS framed as **"we negotiate the seller to cover our commission"** тАФ
never a flat "the seller pays" тАФ and we say **"compensated," not "paid."**

- **"What is it?"** тЖТ *"A short one-page form that just says I'm the agent showing you this home, and it spells out how
  I get compensated, which the seller covers. Not a commitment to keep working with us, it just covers this showing."*
- **"Why do I need to sign?"** тЖТ *"There's a national rule now, part of the recent NAR changes, that agents need a
  signed agreement before showing a home. It actually protects youтАж We always negotiate with the seller to cover our
  commission, so nothing comes out of your pocket."*
- **"Am I locked in?"** тЖТ showing doc: *"this showing only, no obligation after."* ┬╖ 6-month: *"broader so we're not
  re-signing every tour, but you can cancel anytime. We still negotiate the seller to cover our commission, so no risk."*
- **"Do I have to pay you?"** тЖТ *"No! In almost every case we negotiate the seller to cover our commissionтАж if a rare
  case came up where we couldn't, I'd tell you upfront before you committed to anything."*
- **"What if the seller won't cover it?"** тЖТ *"You've got options: we find you another home where they do, or you go it
  alone on that one, but I couldn't represent you on it. Either way, no surprises."*

## The editing surfaces (two, deliberately different)

- **`/ai/prompting`** (`app/(crm)/ai/prompting/page.tsx`) тАФ view + edit each slot; its Save **PUTs the
  LIVE value directly** (`PUT /api/ai/prompts/[key]`, `app/api/ai/prompts/[key]/route.ts:28`).
- **The Test Cockpit's Prompt Editor modal** (`components/settings/test-cockpit/PromptEditorModal.tsx`)
  owns the **draft тЖТ prove тЖТ promote** flow: "Save draft" writes the cockpit draft (live untouched, no
  history row), test against the draft, then "Promote draft to live" does the real PUT.

## The plumbing (how a slot's text actually flows)

- **Version history тАФ `hl_ai_prompt_history`** (mig 0426): every live save appends a row (slot_key, value,
  editor, note). On a slot's **first-ever edit**, the pre-edit original is auto-seeded first
  (`seedOriginal`, `app/api/ai/prompts/[key]/route.ts:83`) so the starting point is never lost.
- **The draft mechanism**: a cockpit draft is stored BESIDE the live value at the same settings key, path
  `<jsonPath>__draft` (`lib/ai/prompt-read.ts:33`). Settings-backed slots only. A live save clears the
  draft so live and draft never diverge silently (route.ts:69-71).
- **The code-constant fallback law**: every compose path reads the live value via
  `readPromptLive(slotKey)` (`lib/ai/prompt-read.ts:14`) and falls back to its old code constant when the
  read returns null тАФ a missing/hiccuping settings row can never break production composing.
- **Honest nuance тАФ the gauntlet gate**: by LAW and practice (┬з02b #5) no prompt change goes live without
  the judged gauntlet green, but the save path does **not yet enforce it** тАФ promote is a manual
  typed-confirm ("No automated suite is wired to this prompt yet", PromptEditorModal.tsx:197). The
  gauntlet gate replaces the confirm when the scenario suites land.

# тХРтХРтХР 02 ┬╖ PROMPTING DOCTRINE тАФ how we write agent prompts тХРтХРтХР

Built from OpenAI's official Realtime prompting guide + cross-vendor research (ElevenLabs, Vapi, Retell)
+ our own live-call scar tissue. Full cited research: `.claude/reports/voice-agent-design-research-2026-07-20.md`.

## The skeleton (OpenAI's, adopted as law for voice agents)

`Role & Objective (as a MISSION with a checklist) тЖТ Personality & Tone (incl. Length / Pacing / Variety) тЖТ
Context (the KB, behaviorally gated) тЖТ Reference Pronunciations тЖТ Tools тЖТ Rules тЖТ Conversation Flow
(states with Goal / sample phrases / Exit-when) тЖТ Safety & Escalation.`

## Our laws (each one paid for in blood)

1. **THE PRESET IS THE LAW.** Start from the provider's official starter config/prompt structure. Never
   hand-tune provider knobs (VAD sensitivities etc.) тАФ every deviation we tried made latency/turn-taking
   WORSE, measured.
2. **The mission is a checklist.** "You are NOT done until you have: тАж" тАФ a described job produces a
   passive agent; a checklist produces drive.
3. **The follow-up-question asymmetry** (from ChatGPT's leaked voice prompt тАФ the "always driving" feel):
   ask ONE single-sentence follow-up when natural; NO question right after answering the caller's direct
   question; never use language that signals the conversation is over.
4. **Ask, never announce.** "Let me get some details" = a stall. Sample phrases ARE the questions тАФ the
   model closely imitates them.
5. **Answer-then-advance.** Caller asks a question тЖТ answer in one sentence тЖТ next intake question in the
   SAME breath.
6. **"Need тАж before" is banned.** Never frame intake as screening ("I need X before the team calls you
   back") тАФ the agent builds the caller's file FOR them.
7. **Variety or robot.** Never the same sentence twice; re-asks must be re-phrased; after two dodges, drop
   it and move on.
8. **Entity capture:** one exact value at a time; digit-by-digit read-back for numbers/addresses;
   re-confirm after corrections; never alter a digit.
9. **Voice-specific:** 1тАУ2 sentences per turn; prosody in words ("speak quickly and warmly") not params;
   spoken-style numbers; ~2,000-token ceiling (latency).
10. **KB without dumping:** answer ONLY from the Context block; not there тЖТ "I don't know" + offer a
    callback; never guess.

# тХРтХРтХР 02b ┬╖ THE CONVERSATION DIRECTOR тАФ code owns the goal (locked 2026-07-21) тХРтХРтХР

The company-defining architecture verdict (2 research rounds тАФ 6 agents over GitHub/commercial
platforms/forums тАФ + ChatGPT's independent review, all converging; full archive:
`.claude/plans/serene-wishing-hamster.md`): **stop scaling the prompt; the runtime owns the
conversation.** The industry consensus (Retell/Bland/Vapi/OpenAI's own reference/Intercom Fin/Sierra):

1. **Per-state prompts** тАФ the model only ever sees the CURRENT state's instructions (our
   triageтЖТplaybook `session.update` hot-swap is OpenAI's own documented pattern, extended per-stage).
2. **Checklists live in CODE, not prompts** тАФ the prompt gets a MISSION ("leave the call so Ana can
   confidently call back"), the runtime tracks the slots. Required-field gates are runtime-enforced.
3. **Turn rules are enforced by GUARDS, not hoped from prompts** тАФ `MyliTurnGuard` (pipecat_bot.py):
   any intake turn ending without a question тЖТ steering message + forced continuation
   (response.create); post-tool silence watchdog. Prompts LOWER the flinch rate; only code
   eliminates it (reproduced live 2026-07-20: the announce-then-silence flinch survived even the
   v2 one-breath prompt until the guard shipped).
4. **Turn-taking is config/runtime, never prompt** тАФ SemanticTurnDetection(eagerness=low) for
   trail-offs + the `wait_for_user` no-op tool (OpenAI's documented hesitation fix).
5. **Eval-gated shipping** тАФ no prompt change goes live without the judged gauntlet green
   (pass^k, repeat runs тАФ single runs lie about stochastic models; Zillow ran 69%тЖТ95% this way).

**Build status (2026-07-21):** the turn-level Director is **BUILT and live** тАФ `MyliTurnGuard`, the
post-tool watchdog, `wait_for_user`, and SemanticTurnDetection all ship in
`apps/myli-voice-agent/pipecat_bot.py` (guard class :371, `wait_for_user` :508, semantic turn :640). The
**slot-tracker checklist + scoring observer** (#2's runtime slot tracking, ┬з07's Observer) is **FUTURE** тАФ
today the checklist still lives in the bucket prompts.

**THE ONE-BREATH RULE** (prompt law #1, in every voice prompt): the acknowledgment and the next
question are ONE breath тАФ a spoken turn is complete ONLY when it ends with exactly one question
(sole exception: the final goodbye, which is SPOKEN before `end_call`, never skipped). Sample
phrases are the strongest instruction-following lever (the model "strongly closely follows sample
phrases" тАФ official guide): every state's samples end in a question.

**ONE BRAIN, MANY MOUTHS** (Zach doctrine 2026-07-20): Myli is ONE persona + ONE per-lead working
memory + ONE rulebook across voice/text/email; the MODELS are swappable per-channel engines under
the brain (phone = gpt-realtime-2.1, text = Claude Sonnet тАФ decided; A/B parked). Deep dive:
`guides/closed-loop-follow-up`. The memory keystone: ┬з06.

# тХРтХРтХР 03 ┬╖ THE VOICE LANE тАФ how the phone persona is COMPOSED тХРтХРтХР

One endpoint owns it: **`GET /api/phone/myli/persona`**
(`apps/houseloop-crm/app/api/phone/myli/persona/route.ts`), called by the Pipecat voice worker at call
start. Myli's phone personality is CONFIG, not code. Runtime/hardware/deploy detail: `guides/ai-voice`.

## Cold hat vs warm hat (the company-line law, Zach 2026-07-15)

The hat is picked by who's calling **AND which line they dialed** (route.ts:44-106):

- The **company / front-door / test line is ALWAYS the cold AI Receptionist** тАФ a total stranger. She
  NEVER name-recognizes a caller just because their number exists somewhere in the CRM.
- The **WARM hat** (greet by first name + full memory) requires BOTH: **(1)** the caller dialed the
  agent's OWN line тАФ a number in `hl_phone_numbers` assigned to this ai_agent (route.ts:66-77) тАФ AND
  **(2)** the matched lead is actually ASSIGNED to her (`assigned_ai_agent_id`, route.ts:103-105).
- Why: the old code flipped warm for ANY lead-matched number on ANY line, so a test lead calling the
  company line got "hey Zach" тАФ dead wrong for a company receptionist.

## GROUND TRUTH injection (route.ts:148-161)

The model has no clock and no caller-ID access тАФ on real calls it invented dates ("Wednesday December
18th") and digits (Zach live-test 2026-07-17). So the route injects the only two per-call facts the voice
layer actually knows: the current date/time (Central) and the caller-ID digits (or "ask and read back
digit by digit" when unavailable). The bot re-prepends the merged ground truth to a bucket when it swaps
prompts mid-call, so the clock + caller-ID survive the swap (route.ts:173-175).

## The composed base (route.ts:163-171)

```
persona = groundTruth
        + role persona            (hl_role.persona, per-agent override wins)
        + role voice doctrine     (hl_role.voice_persona, per-agent override wins)
        + memoryBlock             (warm calls only тАФ composeMyliMemory, ┬з06)
```

with `{{agent.*}}` / `{{ai.*}}` / `{{team.*}}` merge tokens resolved for the acting agent. **The KB block
is intentionally DROPPED from the base** тАФ the receptionist triages first, then the bot swaps in ONE
specialized bucket; keeping the base tiny is the whole latency fix (mig 0419).

## Triage addendum + bucket hot-swap

- The **triage block** comes from the registry (`myli_voice_triage`, mig 0431 тАФ no hidden prompt
  segments) via `readPromptLive` and is returned merged as the `triage` field (route.ts:201-206); the bot
  appends it at call start.
- The **buckets** (`myli_voice_buckets`, merge-token-resolved, route.ts:177-192) are returned as a map;
  the bot hot-swaps the session instructions to base + ONE bucket via `session.update` when the model
  calls `set_caller_type` (pipecat_bot.py:679 тАФ OpenAI's own documented dynamic-flow pattern).
- **`?prompt_source=draft`** overlays each bucket's cockpit draft (and the triage draft) BEFORE merge, so
  the Test Cockpit chats against a draft through the exact same composition path (route.ts:182-192). The
  phone bot never sends the param тАФ live calls unchanged.

The route also returns `voice_enabled` (the master kill switch, from the phone hat's tools) and the
per-capability toggles (capture_caller / set_callback / take_message / flag_spam).

# тХРтХРтХР 04 ┬╖ THE TEXT LANE тАФ how the text system prompt is ASSEMBLED тХРтХРтХР

One file owns the conversational text brain: **`apps/houseloop-crm/lib/ai/lead-conversation.ts`**.

## buildSystemPrompt тАФ the layering (lead-conversation.ts:2018-2088)

```
system = grounding      (RIGHT NOW clock/tz + WHO YOU ARE TEXTING + how-you-search-homes doctrine)
       + agentBlock     (loadDefaultAgentBlock тАФ the acting agent identity)
```

## The instant-ack layer (never a quiet thread)

- **`generateAck`** (:2110) тАФ a fast Haiku micro-call reads the lead's last message + thread position and
  writes ONE fitting buy-time line ("yeah, getting that locked in for you now!") тАФ or SKIPs when buying
  time would read weird. It is armed ONLY when the model actually invokes a slow tool and ~10s pass with
  no reply (:2197-2217) тАФ never a blind timer on small talk.
- **Rotation ban:** the last 8 ack texts persist in `hl_settings.ai_ack_rotation` and are banned from
  reuse, so it can never read canned. **`ACK_VARIANTS`** (:2095) is the static error-fallback pool only.

## THE WHO-SPEAKS GATING CHAIN тАФ `dispatchInboundToAi` (:225)

Every inbound text/iMessage/email walks this chain, in order:

1. **The master switch** (:229 тЖТ `aiEnabledForLead` :212): the ALL-AI failsafe
   `hl_settings ai_master.enabled` (mig 0432, `lib/ai/master-gate.ts` тАФ off beats everything, even the
   pilot list) AND `ai_conversations.enabled` (with the `pilot_lead_ids` live-fire override).
2. **Destination-identity routing** (:237-243): WHO was contacted decides the lane. Inbound landed on an
   AGENT'S identity (their number/email) тЖТ the human owns it and their **AI clone** covers per
   config/SLA тАФ not Myli. Landed on Myli's/company identity (or unknown) тЖТ fall through.
3. **The per-lead mic тАФ `hl_leads.ai_responder`** (mig 0430; :245-259): `'none'` тЖТ NO AI reply, full
   stop. `'clone'` тЖТ the owner's clone lane speaks (no owner тЖТ honest silence). `'myli'` / legacy NULL тЖТ
   Myli's watch lane. The lead-card toggles write this field тАФ they are REAL, not decorative.
4. **The clone master gate** (mig 0431, `hl_settings ai_clones.enabled`): a three-layer AND тАФ master
   toggle ├Ч per-agent `hl_agent_clone_config.enabled` ├Ч the per-lead mic тАФ enforced in
   `lib/agent-runtime/resolve-send-identity.ts:47` (`clonesMasterEnabled`). Master OFF = every clone
   silent; Myli does NOT take over (never a bulk write, so flipping back on restores exact prior config).
5. **Myli's watch lane requirements**: an assigned AI owner + an AI role with a non-empty persona (an
   empty persona logs a VISIBLE `ai_skipped_no_persona` activity row instead of silent death, :283-295).
   Email inbound additionally requires the email dark-gate (┬з05).

Myli's watch is deliberately NOT gated by the per-lead `ai_mode_enabled` escalation flag (:261-266) тАФ
that flag belongs to the SLA/escalation layer; gating on it was exactly why "turn workflows off тЖТ Myli
went silent."

# тХРтХРтХР 07 ┬╖ CONVERSATION STATES & THE SCORING OBSERVER тХРтХРтХР

## The 3-tier state ladder (climb only on evidence)

1. **Single prompt + Conversation Flow section** тАФ phases written in the prompt (Goal / sample phrases /
   Exit-when). Receptionist territory.
2. **Per-state instruction/tool swapping** тАФ the runtime hot-loads instructions per phase. **BUILT тАФ this
   is Myli voice today**: the triage тЖТ bucket hot-swap via `session.update` (┬з03), plus the turn-level
   guards (`MyliTurnGuard`, `wait_for_user`, semantic turn detection тАФ ┬з02b, all live in
   `pipecat_bot.py`). Climb here when calls consistently derail ("context rot").
3. **Hard graph with coded transitions** тАФ compliance-heavy flows only; over-noding sounds robotic.

**The law: the LLM's job is language; the goal lives in code.** Transitions are state mutations signaled
by tool calls тАФ never something the model is trusted to remember.

## The Observer pattern тАФ FUTURE (how lead-qual will work)

Not yet built тАФ the turn guards above are the shipped half of the Director; this is the unshipped half.
Fast model talks naturally. A parallel tracker (deterministic + small LLM extractor) watches the
transcript, owns the slot checklist + score, and steers by injecting context between turns ("You have X
and Y. Next goal: Z. Do not re-ask X."). The talking model never knows it exists. Scoring is ALWAYS
computed by the tracker layer, never the talking model, and persists on the CRM lead record.

## Scoring тАФ FUTURE (the industry pattern, for the lead-qual build)

Rubric-driven (real-estate shape: timeline / motivation / price band / pre-approval / area / has-agent),
0тАУ100 + tier, updated per response, stored as lead fields; threshold crossings fire handoff (hot transfer
/ agent alert). SMS nurture = **CRM-as-state**: each inbound rehydrates from lead fields (slots, score,
next-goal pointer) тЖТ reply тЖТ extractor updates fields тЖТ writeback тЖТ next touch scheduled (behavioral
triggers beat time drips) тАФ maps 1:1 onto Inngest + `hl_leads`.
