### 🗣️ The Voice Law *(governs every buyer-facing message)*

Warm, human, casual, low-pressure, a little self-deprecating — never corporate, never "predator-drone." The model imitates the samples; **the banned list + the samples ARE the prompt.**

- **Banned words** (robotic / high-pressure): "zeroed in," "locked in," "target," "you're after," "is this the one."
- **Never narrate our logistics to the buyer** — no "get Trish lined up"; just *"let me verify with the sellers again."*
- **Never name the showing partner to the buyer until the very end** — they don't know Trish. The partner is revealed
  ONLY at the calendar invite (after signing), never in the acknowledge, buy-time, or close.
- **Never pressure urgency or competition to a buyer** — no "move fast," no "your offer stands out," no "get you ready to move." Buyers fear a huge decision; pushing speed backfires.
- **The tour CTA is always a soft "let me know"** — not "wanna go take a look?" / "worth a peek?" — instead *"let me know if you'd like to go look at it this evening / tomorrow / later this week"* (vary the time).
- **Money is always "we negotiate the seller to cover our commission"** — never "the seller pays"; say **"compensated," not "paid."**
- **Variety is law** — samples she imitates, never the same sentence twice, **or the same emoji back-to-back**. SMS: short, no em dashes.

#### 🏠 Buyer Phase 1 — the full play (LOCKED, Zach 2026-07-23)

**Entry (unified):** ANY property request — tour, property inquiry, contact, general — from **FUB or HouseLoop, any
door — converges to ONE first-touch flow.** (Kills today's dozen near-identical per-door workflows.)

**Two standing laws on the acknowledge — every time:** (1) **always name the platform** they came through ("through
Zillow…"); (2) **always the agent's first + last name** on a first touch ("This is Ana Spradling with The Spradling
Group"), never just the first name.

**The sequence:**

1. **Acknowledge** (as the assigned agent) — two forms:
   - **New lead** (standard first form): *"Hey Sarah, this is Ana Spradling with The Spradling Group. Got your request
     through Zillow to see 123 Main Street tomorrow at 11:00 AM. Does that time still work for you?"*
   - **Existing lead** (drop the full intro, bring context): *"Hey Sarah, Ana again! Just got your request from Zillow
     to see 123 Main Street tomorrow at 11:00 AM. Does that time still work for you?"* — same closing question both ways.
2. **On "yes" — buy time, and bridge straight into the questions** (we keep texting, we don't sign off):
   *"Perfect, let me check with the sellers real quick to make sure it works on their end. While I do that…"*
3. **Behind the scenes — always Myli** texts the **showing partner** (from the lead routing rule; today = Trish):
   *"Hey Trish, it's Myli — got a buyer wanting to see 123 Main at 5. Told them I'm checking your availability."* →
   Trish replies (works / or a new time) → Myli: *"Perfect, I'll arrange it with the buyer and let you know once confirmed."*
4. **Simultaneously — the casual qualification gates** (a **running question bank** — Myli asks the next UNANSWERED
   one at a natural opening, never re-asks, never dumps them all). In order:
   *…are there any other homes in the area you might be interested in looking at?* → *Is this your first time getting
   out to see homes in person, or have you been at it a bit?* → **other agents comes NEXT, bridging off that answer:**
   *"Oh, that's awesome! Have you mainly been looking at open houses, or have you looked at any homes with an agent?"*
   (never let them corner you — you're asking **how** they've shopped, not "do you have an agent") → *What's got you
   looking right now? Outgrowing your place, relocating, first home?* → **rent-or-own:** *"Are you guys currently
   renting, or do you own your place right now?"* (renting → *"when's your lease up?"* — gold intel for timeline) →
   **if another agent →** the representation gate: **"Gotcha — have you signed any type of buyer agreement with them,
   or are you keeping your options open?"** (Zach-locked line). Signed/committed → courteous exit + `has_agent`.
   Options open / nothing signed → proceed, reassure: *"Perfect, just want to make sure we're not stepping on anybody's toes."*
5. **Close the loop** (no agent + gates clear) — **⛔ do NOT name the showing partner yet** (the buyer doesn't know
   Trish; she's revealed only at the calendar invite, step 7): *"Just heard back — 5 o'clock works! 🙌 One quick
   housekeeping thing before then: there's a simple one-page showing agreement I'll send over to sign. It just outlines
   how we're compensated, and we always negotiate that with the seller, so it's nothing out of pocket for you. It only
   covers this one home too, so you're not locked into anything. What's the best email to send it to? Just need it
   signed at least 3 hours before the showing."*
6. **Send the showing agreement** (`send_document`) + its text: *"Hey Sarah! Here's that one-page showing agreement
   [link]. Sign whenever's easy for you, just need it back at least 3 hours before the showing. Any questions, I'm right here!"*
7. **The wrap — TWO messages** (the second doesn't wait for a reply). **This is where the partner is introduced** —
   via the calendar invite + the partner's own hello, never earlier:
   - *"Perfect, thank you Lucy! 😊 Sending that one-page showing agreement over to your email now. Just sign whenever's
     easy and send it back my way."*
   - *"I'm also going to send you a Google Calendar invite, it'll have Trish's contact info in it. Trish is my showing
     partner, she'll reach out to introduce herself and she'll meet you at the house Sunday at noon. Just make sure to
     get that document signed at least 3 hours before the showing so we can confirm everything!"*
   The **Google Calendar invite** (from the assigned agent, both parties on it) carries each other's contact info; **Trish
   also self-introduces**.
8. **Myli texts the showing partner:** *"Hey Trish — got you set up, sent a calendar invite for [buyer]. Details:*
   **Property** · **Date/time** · **Buyer name** · **Buyer phone** · **Note:** *first-time buyer, just started looking,
   searching in [area]."*
9. → **The watching game begins** — buyer confirmed, partner confirmed → **watch for the showing agreement to be
   signed** (Phase 1's real exit gate).

**The laws this phase locks:**

- **Myli is the universal assistant / secretary.** Even when an **AI clone is the talker** to the buyer, **Myli
  always does the orchestration** (partner coordination, sending the agreement, the calendar invite, the partner
  text). Buyer-facing messages go out as the **assigned agent**; Myli works behind the scenes. (Clone-calls-Myli vs
  just-tool-calls: keep it simple — they're tool calls Myli owns.)
- **Unified first-touch** — every property-request door fires the same flow.
- **New tool: `orchestrate_showing_partner`** — contact + arrange the schedule with the routing-rule showing partner.
- **The showing-agreement gate** — a single-page compensation disclosure (NOT the full exclusive buyer agreement)
  satisfies NAR at the *showing* level, signed at least 3 hours before the tour.

**The six columns (locked):** 🎯 confirmed calendared showing + agreement out · 🧰 `text_lead` · `lookup_property` ·
`orchestrate_showing_partner` (NEW) · `send_document` · `create_calendar_invite` · `update_contact` (email) ·
`update_stage` · 🔒 gate = showing agreement signed (≥3 hrs pre-show) · 👻/🎁/❤️ folded into the sequence
(fast, warm, human; the value IS handling the logistics for them).

#### 🔁 Existing lead — the returning buyer (the document escalation ladder)

Phase 1 **absorbs representation.** An existing lead runs the SAME 9-step flow with two differences: we **recall the
relationship** (continuity) instead of re-introducing, and we **escalate the document** — the whole point is *we don't
re-paper them every single tour.*

First move: **look up what they've already signed** (`lookup_signed_agreements` → `{ showing_agreement?, buyer_agency? }`).
That drives the ladder:

| State on file | Action | Framing |
|---|---|---|
| Nothing signed (or brand new) | Send the **single showing agreement** | Runs the new-lead close (steps 5–6) |
| Showing agreement on file **+ touring multiple homes** | **Pivot to the 6-month buyer's agency agreement** | *"one more, and it's the last one you'll sign"* |
| Buyer's agency on file | **No document** | Straight to "you're all set with Trish" |

- **Recall — the GATE (common sense, read WHY they reached out):**
  - **They requested a specific tour → make it about THE HOUSE**, never re-hash the old search (they may have asked for
    something totally different): *"Hey Sarah, just got your request to see 123 Main Street! That one looks amazing,
    love the big backyard on it. Does 5:00 still work, or when's a good time to go see it?"*
  - **They reached out with a general question → the warm welcome-back:** *"Welcome back, Sarah 😊 Last time we talked,
    you were looking for a three-bedroom with a yard for the new puppy. Is that still what you're interested in?"*
  - Either way → *"Perfect, let me check with the sellers again. One quick thing before I do…"* (never "get Trish lined up").
- **The pivot (Zach-locked):** *"You're all set with Trish for 5:30! Last time you signed our single showing agreement.
  This time, since we're out looking at multiple homes, there's just one more document, and this'll be the last one
  you'll need to sign. It's a standing agreement for six months that lets us show you homes without signing something
  new every time. Same terms as before — the seller covers our commission so it's nothing out of pocket, and you can
  cancel at any time."*
- **Already covered:** *"You're all set with Trish for 5:30 — no paperwork this time, you're already covered!"*
- **Exit gate flips:** when we pivot, watch for the **6-month buyer's agency** signature, not the showing doc.

#### ⏱ When they go quiet mid-flow — the low-texter fallback

Not everyone's a texter. After *"does 11 still work?"* three things happen: **they ghost** (→ Ghosting Recovery),
**they answer the tour but skip the questions**, or **they go dark.** The rule: **Trish gets coordinated behind the
scenes no matter what** — silence never stalls the showing. We keep the thread warm on a clock, then **collapse into
the document** when the timer runs out.

- **T+0** — "yes, 11 works" → buy-time line + first question.
- **+30 min, no answer** → one warm nudge that re-asks the **NEXT** gate (not the same one): *"Still waiting to hear
  back from the sellers, should know any minute! While we wait, is this your first time getting out to see homes in
  person, or have you been at it a bit?"*
- **+60 min, still no answer** → stop asking, collapse: *"Hey Sarah, just heard back from the sellers, we're all good
  for 5:30! Quick check first: have you signed a showing or buyer agreement with any other agent? If not, there's just
  a single-page showing agreement that lets us show you this home and outlines how we're compensated, at no cost to you,
  the seller covers it. I'm going to text it over now, and as soon as it's signed you're confirmed!"*

The **signature becomes the gate** — we fold the representation check into the confirmation instead of chasing answers.

#### 🎓 Objection handling (the negotiate framing)

Answered from the knowledge base, calm and plain-English, never defensive. **Through-line: it protects you, it's
required, it costs you nothing.** Money is ALWAYS framed as **"we negotiate the seller to cover our commission"** —
never a flat "the seller pays" — and we say **"compensated," not "paid."**

- **"What is it?"** → *"A short one-page form that just says I'm the agent showing you this home, and it spells out how
  I get compensated, which the seller covers. Not a commitment to keep working with us, it just covers this showing."*
- **"Why do I need to sign?"** → *"There's a national rule now, part of the recent NAR changes, that agents need a
  signed agreement before showing a home. It actually protects you… We always negotiate with the seller to cover our
  commission, so nothing comes out of your pocket."*
- **"Am I locked in?"** → showing doc: *"this showing only, no obligation after."* · 6-month: *"broader so we're not
  re-signing every tour, but you can cancel anytime. We still negotiate the seller to cover our commission, so no risk."*
- **"Do I have to pay you?"** → *"No! In almost every case we negotiate the seller to cover our commission… if a rare
  case came up where we couldn't, I'd tell you upfront before you committed to anything."*
- **"What if the seller won't cover it?"** → *"You've got options: we find you another home where they do, or you go it
  alone on that one, but I couldn't represent you on it. Either way, no surprises."*

### 🔀 Phase 2 — Migrate + Recap (the post-showing-setup email)

The moment the calendar invite is out, the AI sends a **recap email** from the assigned agent. Three jobs at once:
**(1) recap** the text thread so nothing's lost, **(2) migrate** them off the portal — create their HouseLoop account
+ a saved search in their area, **(3) set the follow-up** ("I'll follow up after you meet Trish"). *The migration rides
the recap.* Tools: `send_email` · `create_account` · `create_saved_search` · `search_homes`.

> **Subject: You're all set for 123 Main Street 🏡 (+ a few homes for you)**
>
> Hey Sarah, great texting with you just now! I just sent over the Google Calendar invite for your showing at 123 Main
> Street tomorrow at 5:30 with Trish — you've each got the other's info there.
>
> Quick recap: ✅ Showing confirmed · 📄 just waiting on that one-page showing agreement (signed at least 3 hours before) ·
> 📝 what you're looking for: your first home around Overland Park, single-family in the low-to-mid $200s.
>
> One more thing that'll help you: I set you up on HouseLoop, our home-search platform. It sometimes updates a little
> quicker than Zillow and catches homes Zillow doesn't have. I set up a search for single-family in Overland Park in the
> $200–250k range — 27 match right now. Here's your link 👉 [link]; tweak it anytime.
>
> Enjoy the showing! I'll follow up after you meet with Trish tomorrow to hear how it went. — Ana

**Why it matters:** the account is the migration AND the measurement — on follow-up we verify *"did you check the homes
I sent?"* and can see their visit history on the lead.

### 🔁 Phase 3 — The Showing & Feedback Loop *(recurring, every showing)*

The loop most teams drop: **Trish texts Myli the feedback**, Myli turns it into a **game plan**, and follows up with the
client **the day after**. This is where the relationship is won or lost.

1. **Trish → Myli debrief:** Myli prompts, Trish reports (*"loved the kitchen, yard smaller than she hoped, felt a touch
   overpriced, still looking"*), Myli logs it to the lead.
2. **Myli builds the game plan:** adjust the saved search, queue 2–3 comps that fix the objection (bigger yard), note it.
3. **Day-after follow-up (client):** *"Hey Sarah! How'd 123 Main feel in person? Trish mentioned you loved the kitchen. I
   pulled a few similar homes with bigger yards and dropped them in your HouseLoop search. This one caught my eye — same
   kind of kitchen as Main Street, but a bigger backyard 👉 [link]. Let me know if you'd like to go see it sometime
   tomorrow or later this week. Curious what you think!"*

### 🔀 Phase 4 — Behavioral Nurture Engine *(always-on, after the appointment)*

The watcher reads behavior and branches. Behavior-triggered gates (lender, offer) fire from here.

- **🔥 Engaged** (browsing / requesting tours) → ride it: *"Love it! Let me verify with the sellers again. While I do
  that…"* → back into the showing flow, asking the next unanswered question from the bank.
- **🥶 Quiet** (not looking, no tour requests) → the **handcrafted one-home send** every couple days (never a digest):
  - *Database check-in:* *"Hey Sarah, no rush at all 😊 I was just going through my clients and realized we hadn't talked
    in a bit — didn't want to drop the ball in case you're thinking about moving sooner rather than later. I dug up a
    house that caught my eye. Might be totally off base if things have changed, but let me know what you think."*
  - *Home send:* *"The sellers just put this one up over on Maple Street in Overland Park — 4 bed with a fenced yard for
    the pup. Thought of you 👉 [link]. Let me know if you'd like to go take a look this evening or later this week."*
  - *Timeline deepen + rent/own:* *"No rush at all — are you thinking sometime this summer, or just keeping an eye out for
    the right one?"* · *"Are you guys currently renting, or do you own your place right now?"*
- **⏳ Triage mode (early-timeline buyer):** if qual shows they're early (just re-signed a lease, 6+ mo out), **don't show
  houses every weekend** — a **human** calls with a game plan: *"It's still a little early to be touring every weekend —
  we usually ramp around 90 days out because of how the closing process times up. For now, look online, I'll keep sending
  you homes, and I'll send the weekend open houses — you can knock out 3–4 in an afternoon, no appointment needed. And if
  you fall in love with one at an open house and want to write, just text me or call Trish."*

### The Seller Journey — toward a signed listing agreement *(proposed skeleton)*

- ⬜ **Phase 1 — New Lead / Valuation Request** — 🎯 set the listing appointment.
- ⬜ **Phase 2 — Representation** — 🎯 become their listing agent · already-listed → courteous exit.
- ⬜ **Phase 3 — The Valuation Hook** — 🎯 deliver the home value / CMA teaser (the reason they raised a hand).
- ⬜ **Phase 4 — Qualify** — 🎯 motivation + timeline + price expectation.
- ⬜ **Phase 5 — Listing Appointment + Agreement** 🔒 — 🎯 pre-listing packet → listing agreement signed.
- ⬜ **Phase 6 — Behavioral Nurture** — 🎯 equity / market updates until they're ready.

### Ghosting Recovery *(the default when a lead goes quiet in ANY phase — not a branch)*

- ⬜ **Cadence** *(default to debate: 5–7 touches / 4–6 weeks, then long-term behavioral)* · ⬜ **Angles** (value · market · pattern-interrupt · breakup · new-listing) · ⬜ **The breakup/takeaway text** (loss aversion) · ⬜ **Buyer ghost vs seller ghost** · ⬜ **Channel escalation** (text→email→call→behavioral) · ⬜ **The hard exit** (TCPA 8am–8pm; a trigger, not a calendar).

### The Value Ladder *(every touch is a gift, never a "just checking in" — behavioral beats time ~30 percent)*

- ⬜ **Buyer value rungs** (① market data → ⑥ lender intro + pre-approval) · ⬜ **Seller value rungs** (① equity update → ⑥ refreshed CMA → listing consult) · ⬜ **Behavioral triggers** (which micro-signal fires which drop) · ⬜ **The gift-vs-nag line** we never cross.

# ═══ 02 · PROMPTING DOCTRINE — how we write agent prompts ═══

Built from OpenAI's official Realtime prompting guide + cross-vendor research (ElevenLabs, Vapi, Retell)
+ our own live-call scar tissue. Full cited research: `.claude/reports/voice-agent-design-research-2026-07-20.md`.

## The skeleton (OpenAI's, adopted as law for voice agents)

`Role & Objective (as a MISSION with a checklist) → Personality & Tone (incl. Length / Pacing / Variety) →
Context (the KB, behaviorally gated) → Reference Pronunciations → Tools → Rules → Conversation Flow
(states with Goal / sample phrases / Exit-when) → Safety & Escalation.`

## Our laws (each one paid for in blood)

1. **THE PRESET IS THE LAW.** Start from the provider's official starter config/prompt structure. Never
   hand-tune provider knobs (VAD sensitivities etc.) — every deviation we tried made latency/turn-taking
   WORSE, measured.
2. **The mission is a checklist.** "You are NOT done until you have: …" — a described job produces a
   passive agent; a checklist produces drive.
3. **The follow-up-question asymmetry** (from ChatGPT's leaked voice prompt — the "always driving" feel):
   ask ONE single-sentence follow-up when natural; NO question right after answering the caller's direct
   question; never use language that signals the conversation is over.
4. **Ask, never announce.** "Let me get some details" = a stall. Sample phrases ARE the questions — the
   model closely imitates them.
5. **Answer-then-advance.** Caller asks a question → answer in one sentence → next intake question in the
   SAME breath.
6. **"Need … before" is banned.** Never frame intake as screening ("I need X before the team calls you
   back") — the agent builds the caller's file FOR them.
7. **Variety or robot.** Never the same sentence twice; re-asks must be re-phrased; after two dodges, drop
   it and move on.
8. **Entity capture:** one exact value at a time; digit-by-digit read-back for numbers/addresses;
   re-confirm after corrections; never alter a digit.
9. **Voice-specific:** 1–2 sentences per turn; prosody in words ("speak quickly and warmly") not params;
   spoken-style numbers; ~2,000-token ceiling (latency).
10. **KB without dumping:** answer ONLY from the Context block; not there → "I don't know" + offer a
    callback; never guess.

# ═══ 02b · THE CONVERSATION DIRECTOR — code owns the goal (locked 2026-07-21) ═══

The company-defining architecture verdict (2 research rounds — 6 agents over GitHub/commercial
platforms/forums — + ChatGPT's independent review, all converging; full archive:
`.claude/plans/serene-wishing-hamster.md`): **stop scaling the prompt; the runtime owns the
conversation.** The industry consensus (Retell/Bland/Vapi/OpenAI's own reference/Intercom Fin/Sierra):

1. **Per-state prompts** — the model only ever sees the CURRENT state's instructions (our
   triage→playbook `session.update` hot-swap is OpenAI's own documented pattern, extended per-stage).
2. **Checklists live in CODE, not prompts** — the prompt gets a MISSION ("leave the call so Ana can
   confidently call back"), the runtime tracks the slots. Required-field gates are runtime-enforced.
3. **Turn rules are enforced by GUARDS, not hoped from prompts** — `MyliTurnGuard` (pipecat_bot.py):
   any intake turn ending without a question → steering message + forced continuation
   (response.create); post-tool silence watchdog. Prompts LOWER the flinch rate; only code
   eliminates it (reproduced live 2026-07-20: the announce-then-silence flinch survived even the
   v2 one-breath prompt until the guard shipped).
4. **Turn-taking is config/runtime, never prompt** — SemanticTurnDetection(eagerness=low) for
   trail-offs + the `wait_for_user` no-op tool (OpenAI's documented hesitation fix).
5. **Eval-gated shipping** — no prompt change goes live without the judged gauntlet green
   (pass^k, repeat runs — single runs lie about stochastic models; Zillow ran 69%→95% this way).

**Build status (2026-07-21):** the turn-level Director is **BUILT and live** — `MyliTurnGuard`, the
post-tool watchdog, `wait_for_user`, and SemanticTurnDetection all ship in
`apps/myli-voice-agent/pipecat_bot.py` (guard class :371, `wait_for_user` :508, semantic turn :640). The
**slot-tracker checklist + scoring observer** (#2's runtime slot tracking, §07's Observer) is **FUTURE** —
today the checklist still lives in the bucket prompts.

**THE ONE-BREATH RULE** (prompt law #1, in every voice prompt): the acknowledgment and the next
question are ONE breath — a spoken turn is complete ONLY when it ends with exactly one question
(sole exception: the final goodbye, which is SPOKEN before `end_call`, never skipped). Sample
phrases are the strongest instruction-following lever (the model "strongly closely follows sample
phrases" — official guide): every state's samples end in a question.

**ONE BRAIN, MANY MOUTHS** (Zach doctrine 2026-07-20): Myli is ONE persona + ONE per-lead working
memory + ONE rulebook across voice/text/email; the MODELS are swappable per-channel engines under
the brain (phone = gpt-realtime-2.1, text = Claude Sonnet — decided; A/B parked). Deep dive:
`guides/closed-loop-follow-up`. The memory keystone: §06.

# ═══ 03 · THE VOICE LANE — how the phone persona is COMPOSED ═══

One endpoint owns it: **`GET /api/phone/myli/persona`**
(`apps/houseloop-crm/app/api/phone/myli/persona/route.ts`), called by the Pipecat voice worker at call
start. Myli's phone personality is CONFIG, not code. Runtime/hardware/deploy detail: `guides/ai-voice`.

## Cold hat vs warm hat (the company-line law, Zach 2026-07-15)

The hat is picked by who's calling **AND which line they dialed** (route.ts:44-106):

- The **company / front-door / test line is ALWAYS the cold AI Receptionist** — a total stranger. She
  NEVER name-recognizes a caller just because their number exists somewhere in the CRM.
- The **WARM hat** (greet by first name + full memory) requires BOTH: **(1)** the caller dialed the
  agent's OWN line — a number in `hl_phone_numbers` assigned to this ai_agent (route.ts:66-77) — AND
  **(2)** the matched lead is actually ASSIGNED to her (`assigned_ai_agent_id`, route.ts:103-105).
- Why: the old code flipped warm for ANY lead-matched number on ANY line, so a test lead calling the
  company line got "hey Zach" — dead wrong for a company receptionist.

## GROUND TRUTH injection (route.ts:148-161)

The model has no clock and no caller-ID access — on real calls it invented dates ("Wednesday December
18th") and digits (Zach live-test 2026-07-17). So the route injects the only two per-call facts the voice
layer actually knows: the current date/time (Central) and the caller-ID digits (or "ask and read back
digit by digit" when unavailable). The bot re-prepends the merged ground truth to a bucket when it swaps
prompts mid-call, so the clock + caller-ID survive the swap (route.ts:173-175).

## The composed base (route.ts:163-171)

```
persona = groundTruth
        + role persona            (hl_role.persona, per-agent override wins)
        + role voice doctrine     (hl_role.voice_persona, per-agent override wins)
        + memoryBlock             (warm calls only — composeMyliMemory, §06)
```

with `{{agent.*}}` / `{{ai.*}}` / `{{team.*}}` merge tokens resolved for the acting agent. **The KB block
is intentionally DROPPED from the base** — the receptionist triages first, then the bot swaps in ONE
specialized bucket; keeping the base tiny is the whole latency fix (mig 0419).

## Triage addendum + bucket hot-swap

- The **triage block** comes from the registry (`myli_voice_triage`, mig 0431 — no hidden prompt
  segments) via `readPromptLive` and is returned merged as the `triage` field (route.ts:201-206); the bot
  appends it at call start.
- The **buckets** (`myli_voice_buckets`, merge-token-resolved, route.ts:177-192) are returned as a map;
  the bot hot-swaps the session instructions to base + ONE bucket via `session.update` when the model
  calls `set_caller_type` (pipecat_bot.py:679 — OpenAI's own documented dynamic-flow pattern).
- **`?prompt_source=draft`** overlays each bucket's cockpit draft (and the triage draft) BEFORE merge, so
  the Test Cockpit chats against a draft through the exact same composition path (route.ts:182-192). The
  phone bot never sends the param — live calls unchanged.

The route also returns `voice_enabled` (the master kill switch, from the phone hat's tools) and the
per-capability toggles (capture_caller / set_callback / take_message / flag_spam).

# ═══ 04 · THE TEXT LANE — how the text system prompt is ASSEMBLED ═══

One file owns the conversational text brain: **`apps/houseloop-crm/lib/ai/lead-conversation.ts`**.

## buildSystemPrompt — the layering (lead-conversation.ts:2018-2088)

```
system = grounding      (RIGHT NOW clock/tz + WHO YOU ARE TEXTING + how-you-search-homes doctrine)
       + agentBlock     (loadDefaultAgentBlock — the acting agent identity)
       + stagesBlock    (the lead's live pipeline stages → drives appointment type)
       + memory         (composeMyliMemory — §06, the one-brain keystone)
       + persona        (role persona + per-agent override + the role's KB docs, smart-fields resolved)
```

Unlike the voice base, the text lane DOES inject the role's Knowledge Base docs into the persona stack
(:2029-2038) — latency physics differ; there is no per-turn re-read tax on text.

## The instant-ack layer (never a quiet thread)

- **`generateAck`** (:2110) — a fast Haiku micro-call reads the lead's last message + thread position and
  writes ONE fitting buy-time line ("yeah, getting that locked in for you now!") — or SKIPs when buying
  time would read weird. It is armed ONLY when the model actually invokes a slow tool and ~10s pass with
  no reply (:2197-2217) — never a blind timer on small talk.
- **Rotation ban:** the last 8 ack texts persist in `hl_settings.ai_ack_rotation` and are banned from
  reuse, so it can never read canned. **`ACK_VARIANTS`** (:2095) is the static error-fallback pool only.

## THE WHO-SPEAKS GATING CHAIN — `dispatchInboundToAi` (:225)

Every inbound text/iMessage/email walks this chain, in order:

1. **The master switch** (:229 → `aiEnabledForLead` :212): the ALL-AI failsafe
   `hl_settings ai_master.enabled` (mig 0432, `lib/ai/master-gate.ts` — off beats everything, even the
   pilot list) AND `ai_conversations.enabled` (with the `pilot_lead_ids` live-fire override).
2. **Destination-identity routing** (:237-243): WHO was contacted decides the lane. Inbound landed on an
   AGENT'S identity (their number/email) → the human owns it and their **AI clone** covers per
   config/SLA — not Myli. Landed on Myli's/company identity (or unknown) → fall through.
3. **The per-lead mic — `hl_leads.ai_responder`** (mig 0430; :245-259): `'none'` → NO AI reply, full
   stop. `'clone'` → the owner's clone lane speaks (no owner → honest silence). `'myli'` / legacy NULL →
   Myli's watch lane. The lead-card toggles write this field — they are REAL, not decorative.
4. **The clone master gate** (mig 0431, `hl_settings ai_clones.enabled`): a three-layer AND — master
   toggle × per-agent `hl_agent_clone_config.enabled` × the per-lead mic — enforced in
   `lib/agent-runtime/resolve-send-identity.ts:47` (`clonesMasterEnabled`). Master OFF = every clone
   silent; Myli does NOT take over (never a bulk write, so flipping back on restores exact prior config).
5. **Myli's watch lane requirements**: an assigned AI owner + an AI role with a non-empty persona (an
   empty persona logs a VISIBLE `ai_skipped_no_persona` activity row instead of silent death, :283-295).
   Email inbound additionally requires the email dark-gate (§05).

Myli's watch is deliberately NOT gated by the per-lead `ai_mode_enabled` escalation flag (:261-266) —
that flag belongs to the SLA/escalation layer; gating on it was exactly why "turn workflows off → Myli
went silent."

# ═══ 05 · THE FIRST-TOUCH / EMAIL LANE ═══

One function owns the first-touch brief: **`buildFirstTouchBrief`**
(`apps/houseloop-crm/lib/workflows/first-touch-brief.ts:160`). Both the live workflow node
(`execute-step.ts` → ai_message) AND the preview harness call it, so a preview is byte-for-byte what gets

## Contract selection order (:176-179)

```
explicit override (cockpit re-run-with-draft)
  > tour_first_touch_contract (live slot, when the request is a tour)
  > first_touch_contract (live slot)
  > CONTEXT_FIRST_CONTRACT (the code constant — the never-break fallback)
```

## What gets stacked into the brief (:217-228)

- **The contract** (above) — the dominant, in-the-moment instruction: greeting → who you are → the REAL
  relationship (only if the dossier supports it; never invented) → the request by ADDRESS → ONE question.
  Tour first touches deliberately withhold the relationship line from message 1.
- **`intentGuidance`** (:127) per resolved intent: tour_request (acknowledge the EXACT time, never re-ask),
  property/FUB inquiry, seller_inquiry, contact_request.
- **The relationship line** (:100) pulled straight from `hl_lead_dossier` + `hl_lead_facts` so it sits
  beside the contract, not 40 facts deep in the system prompt.
- **Platform + brand lines** (:185-200): the platform the request came through ("through Zillow") and the
  company name the responder introduces with — from the lead's ROUTING RULE (mig 0436), never hardcoded.
- **`formatRuleFor(channel)`** (:54): **SMS physics** for text (aim under 160 GSM-7 chars = one billed
  segment; 320 hard ceiling; no em dashes / curly quotes — they force UCS-2 and triple carrier cost) vs
  the email body shape (2–4 short paragraphs, no subject/signature — added automatically).
- **`STYLE_RULE`** (:39 — casual tone from word choice, never dropped capitalization) and
  **`NO_REASONING`** (:46 — output ONLY the message; a leaked chain-of-thought to a real lead is a fire).

## THE EMAIL TRUTH — no separate email slot

There is **no email prompt slot and no separate email brain**. Email reuses the text brain end-to-end:
the same `dispatchInboundToAi` chain, the same `buildSystemPrompt`, the same contracts — with
`formatRuleFor('email')` swapping the output shape. Email auto-reply ships **DARK** behind
`hl_settings ai_conversations.email_enabled` (explicit `true` to activate;
lead-conversation.ts:273-277) until the e2e proves it. Texts unaffected.

**`composeMyliMemory(leadId)`** (`apps/houseloop-crm/lib/ai/myli-memory.ts:18`) is the canonical per-lead
memory composer — the working-memory layer of ONE BRAIN, MANY MOUTHS. Every channel that speaks for Myli

- **Voice** — the persona route injects it as the warm-call memory block

# ═══ 07 · CONVERSATION STATES & THE SCORING OBSERVER ═══

## The 3-tier state ladder (climb only on evidence)

1. **Single prompt + Conversation Flow section** — phases written in the prompt (Goal / sample phrases /
   Exit-when). Receptionist territory.
2. **Per-state instruction/tool swapping** — the runtime hot-loads instructions per phase. **BUILT — this
   is Myli voice today**: the triage → bucket hot-swap via `session.update` (§03), plus the turn-level
   guards (`MyliTurnGuard`, `wait_for_user`, semantic turn detection — §02b, all live in
   `pipecat_bot.py`). Climb here when calls consistently derail ("context rot").
3. **Hard graph with coded transitions** — compliance-heavy flows only; over-noding sounds robotic.

**The law: the LLM's job is language; the goal lives in code.** Transitions are state mutations signaled
by tool calls — never something the model is trusted to remember.

## The Observer pattern — FUTURE (how lead-qual will work)

Not yet built — the turn guards above are the shipped half of the Director; this is the unshipped half.
Fast model talks naturally. A parallel tracker (deterministic + small LLM extractor) watches the
transcript, owns the slot checklist + score, and steers by injecting context between turns ("You have X
and Y. Next goal: Z. Do not re-ask X."). The talking model never knows it exists. Scoring is ALWAYS
computed by the tracker layer, never the talking model, and persists on the CRM lead record.

## Scoring — FUTURE (the industry pattern, for the lead-qual build)

Rubric-driven (real-estate shape: timeline / motivation / price band / pre-approval / area / has-agent),
0–100 + tier, updated per response, stored as lead fields; threshold crossings fire handoff (hot transfer
/ agent alert). SMS nurture = **CRM-as-state**: each inbound rehydrates from lead fields (slots, score,
next-goal pointer) → reply → extractor updates fields → writeback → next touch scheduled (behavioral
triggers beat time drips) — maps 1:1 onto Inngest + `hl_leads`.
