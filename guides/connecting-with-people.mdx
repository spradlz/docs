<Info>
**What this is.** The **Connecting With People Framework** is Spradling Group's relationship-first conversation
method (originally a Hatch Coaching handout, adapted for HouseLoop). This page documents the framework itself
**and** the architecture for how **Myli** runs it on an inbound **tour request** — the AI Follow-Up flagship
conversation. Source framework lives in the "Spradling Group Leadership" shared drive
(`Connecting With People Framework.pdf` + video). Interactive architecture artifact + the in-app mobile page:
see [Where to see it](#where-to-see-it).
</Info>

## The thesis

**A tour request is not a door. It's the start of a relationship.** The old way answers the *what* ("yes, 5pm
works"). Myli answers the what **and** earns the *who* — she learns the person, reflects their story back, and
gives them a plan. Same inbound request, a relationship instead of a transaction.

## The framework — three moves

The method is two halves: the first two moves **ask** to understand; the third **gives** — reflecting the person
back and serving them a plan. Under each move is the verb that names what you're doing.

| Move | Verb | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · Curiosity** | *Vett* | **Motivation** (what's driving the move) · **Ability** (rent/own, lease, sell-first?) · **Agent** (seen homes in person or just online?) · **Timeline** (ideal move date) |
| **2 · Connection** | *Dig Deep* | **Their Story** (what they do, how long in the area) · **Experience** (first-time? lender yet?) · **Home Details** (non-negotiables, location, beds/baths) · **Red Flags** (long search, other agents, credit → "tell me more about that") |
| **3 · Collaboration** | *Serve* | **RE-their story** (rephrase, reflect, remind) · **Herd effect** ("I work with a lot of buyers just like you") · **Game Plan** (the concrete next steps) |

## How Myli runs it on a tour request

### She never opens cold

The tour request arrives with a home, a time, and a person who already has a history. Before Myli says a word she
has read:

| What she knows | Source |
|---|---|
| The home &amp; requested time | `hl_pixel_event` / FUB webhook |
| Contact (name, phone, email) | `hl_leads` · `fub.people` |
| Every prior property inquiry | `fub.events` |
| The deep Zillow browse trail | `fub.zillow_home_activity` |
| Prior texts / calls / notes | `fub.text_messages` · `fub.calls` · `fub.notes` |
| Lender / stage / tags | `fub.people` |
| Seller side + showing rules | `Listing` · TC loop |
| Area showing partner | HL team roster (rotation) |
| Exclusive-Right-to-Show status | e-sign (Documenso) |

Because she walks in already knowing, her **Curiosity** move is a *reflection*, not an interrogation: "I can see
you've been looking at a lot of homes across the Northland and south KC — let me actually help you win one." A high
inquiry + browse count is itself the framework's **Red Flag** (long search, likely multiple agents) — and her
biggest opening.

### The conversation, beat by beat

1. **Open on the ask — serve first.** Confirm the *what* instantly ("I can absolutely help you see it — let me
   check with the sellers that 5:00 works"), which earns permission to keep talking.
2. **Curiosity · Vett.** Motivation + Ability + Timeline in one breath — is this a ready buyer, and how fast?
3. **Connection · Dig Deep.** Experience + the lender question ("…or is that something we should help line up?" —
   a gap becomes a service, not a gate).
4. **Connection · Dig Deep — the red flag.** Name the long search gently, surface whether they're already
   committed to another agent (this decides the ERTS framing).
5. **Collaboration · Serve — reflect.** RE-their story (rephrase, reflect, remind) + herd effect. The emotional
   turn: they feel understood.
6. **Collaboration · Serve — the Game Plan.** The HouseLoop version (below).

### The Game Plan (the HouseLoop "Serve" move)

Not a Zoom call — the concrete steps that get them into the house **and** capture the agency relationship before
the showing. Each promise is a real system action, and two are **gates**:

1. **Text the Exclusive Right-to-Show (ERTS) link** — Myli fires the e-sign; captures agency **before** the tour.
2. **Confirm the time with the sellers** — *gate*: our listing → seller/TC; co-broke → listing agent's showing
   system. Nothing confirms to the buyer until this returns yes.
3. **Both gates green → confirm the showing** — ERTS signed **and** seller confirmed → Myli texts the green light.
4. **Warm-hand to the showing partner** — Myli briefs the area partner (e.g. Trish) with the full context; the
   partner introduces herself and meets the buyer at the home. The lead feels **upgraded**, never handed off.

### Decision tree — where it forks

- **Phone vs no phone** → full text conversation, or email-only variant.
- **First-time vs experienced** → lean into herd effect + lender help, or shorten Curiosity and respect their time.
- **Already working with an agent** → **do not poach**; be gracious, flag to a human.
- **Seller declines the time** → offer the nearest workable window, re-confirm both sides.
- **ERTS not signed by cutoff** → one warm nudge; never lose the tour over paperwork (partner can carry a paper form).
- **Reply needs a human** (anger, legal, price negotiation) → capture essentials, hand to the owner/partner.

## What's live vs. what this needs

<Info>
**This is an architecture, not a shipped conversation brain yet.** The **data spine** (FUB history, the browse
trail, the pixel tour-request signal, listing data, Myli's context builder) and the **plumbing** (tour-request
workflow, e-sign, showing-partner roster, Myli's iMessage/SMS send path) all exist today. The **new work** is the
conversation state machine that sequences them by the framework (Curiosity → Connection → Collaboration) and reads
each reply to pick the branch, plus tying the two gates (seller-confirm + ERTS-signed) into one "showing
confirmed" condition.
</Info>
