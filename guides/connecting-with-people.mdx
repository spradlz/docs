## The thesis

**A tour request isn't a door. It's the start of a relationship.**

The old way answers the *what* ("yes, 5pm works"). We answer the what **and** earn the *who* — learn the person,
reflect their story back, and give them a plan. Same inbound request, a relationship instead of a transaction.

The reference case throughout: a real lead (Msale Olongo, Zillow, FUB #45774) — 8 property inquiries in a month,
841 Zillow browse events on file, texting "I want to see this house at 5."

## The framework — three moves

Two halves: the first two moves **ask** to understand; the third **gives** — reflecting the person back and
serving them a plan. Under each move is the verb that names what you're doing.

| Move | Verb | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · Curiosity** | *Vett* | **Motivation** — what's got them wanting to move · **Ability** — rent or own, lease vs month-to-month, need to sell first? · **Agent** — seen homes in person, or just online? · **Timeline** — in an ideal world, when's the perfect move? |
| **2 · Connection** | *Dig Deep* | **Their Story** — what they do, how long in the area · **Experience** — first-time buyer? spoken to a lender yet? · **Home Details** — non-negotiables, location, beds/baths · **Red Flags** — long search, other agents, credit → "tell me more about that" |
| **3 · Collaboration** | *Serve* | **RE-their story** — rephrase, reflect, remind · **Herd effect** — "I work with a lot of buyers just like you" · **Game Plan** — the HouseLoop version: the showing agreement + the handoff |

## Before she says a word — what the AI already knows

She never opens cold. The tour request arrives with a home, a time, and a person who already has a history in
Follow Up Boss and a browse trail the scraper recovered. This is the raw material for Curiosity — she **reflects
what she sees instead of interrogating**.

- **The request itself** (`hl_pixel` / FUB): the listing they asked to tour — address, price, beds/baths, photos,
  status; the requested showing window ("today at 5:00"); the contact.
- **Their Follow Up Boss history** (`fub.events`): every prior property inquiry — in the reference case, 8 in
  30 days across KC-metro MO suburbs → a picture of price band + area; source = Zillow; no lender or agent noted.
- **The deep browse trail** (`fub.zillow_home_activity`): 841 browse events the scraper recovered — the TRUE
  search: beds, price ceiling, neighborhoods, repeat-viewed homes.
- **The seller side** (`Listing` / TC): who the listing agent is, showing instructions, lockbox/appointment rules;
  whether WE represent the seller or it's co-broke; the showing partner on rotation for that area (e.g. Trisha).

**The unlock:** 8 inquiries + 841 browse events means the buyer has been shopping hard, probably across several
agents. That's a **Red Flag** in the framework — and the AI's biggest opening. She doesn't say "what are you
looking for?" She says *"I can see you've been at this a while across the Northland and south KC — let me
actually help you win one."*

## The conversation, beat by beat — with the exact words

Each beat shows what she says, which framework move it is, and where the data comes from. Bolded values are live
merge fields pulled per-lead. She never dumps all of this — **she reads the reply and moves.**

### Beat 01 — Open on the ask, earn the right to keep talking (Door → Serve first)

> Hi **Msale**! This is Myli with the Spradling Group. I see you'd love to see **7430 Manchester Ave** **today
> at 5:00** — I can absolutely help make that happen. Let me check with the sellers to make sure that time works
> on their end, and I'll lock it in for you. Real quick while I do that —

**Why:** lead with a yes, not a form. She confirms the *what* instantly so the person relaxes, then earns
permission to ask. The seller-check is a real dependency AND a natural reason to keep them on.
Pulls: `event.home_address` · `event.requested_time` · `lead.first_name`

### Beat 02 — Curiosity: motivation and timeline (Curiosity · Vett)

> — tell me a little about your situation. Are you renting right now, or would you need to sell a place before
> you buy? And in a perfect world, when would you love to be in your next home?

**Why:** Motivation + Ability + Timeline in one breath. This vets whether they're a ready buyer or a browser —
and how fast. She already suspects "active" (8 inquiries) so she's confirming, not fishing.
Pulls: `fub.events[].type` · infer: `active_shopper`

### Beat 03 — Connection: their story and the lender question (Connection · Dig Deep)

> Love it. Is this your first time buying, or have you done this before? And have you had a chance to talk to a
> lender yet — or is that something we should help line up?

**Why:** Experience + the single most important qualifying question in real estate (financing). "Or is that
something we should help line up" turns a gap into a service, not a gate.
Pulls: `fub.notes` (lender?) · writes: `lead.facts.lender_status`

### Beat 04 — Connection: read the red flag, dig deeper (Connection · Dig Deep)

> I can see you've been looking at quite a few homes lately — **Birchwood, Old Pike, a few in the Northland**.
> Have you been working with another agent, or kind of doing it on your own so far? Tell me a little about how
> the search has gone.

**Why:** the 841 browse events + 8 inquiries ARE the red flag — long search, likely multiple agents. Naming it
(gently) shows she's paying attention and surfaces whether they're already committed to someone. Their answer
decides the showing-agreement framing in the Game Plan.
Pulls: `zillow_home_activity` (top areas) · `fub.events` (inquiry addresses) · branch: `has_agent?`

### Beat 05 — Collaboration: reflect their story back (Collaboration · Serve)

> So it sounds like **you've been at this a couple months, doing it mostly solo, and you're ready to move on the
> right one fast**. That's exactly the person we're built for — I work with a lot of buyers in your shoes across
> **south KC and the Northland**, and the difference is having someone actually in your corner.

**Why:** RE-their story (rephrase, reflect, remind) + herd effect. Every merged value is composed live from what
they just said + FUB history. This is the emotional turn — they feel understood.
Pulls: `ai.compose(reflection)` · `lead.facts.*`

### Beat 06 — Collaboration: the HouseLoop Game Plan (Collaboration · Serve)

> Here's exactly what happens next so you can see **7430 Manchester** today:
> 1. I'm texting you a quick **showing agreement** — it's a 2-minute e-sign that lets us officially show you the house.
> 2. I'm confirming with the sellers right now that 5:00 works.
> 3. The second I hear back, I'll text you the green light.
> 4. Your showing partner is **Trisha** — she'll reach out to introduce herself and meet you at the house at **5:30**.
>
> Sound good?

**Why:** the framework's "Game Plan" move, rebuilt for HouseLoop. Not a Zoom call — the concrete next steps that
get them into the house AND capture the agency relationship (the showing agreement) before the showing. Every
step is a real system action.
Pulls: `esign.send` · `tc.seller_confirm()` · `team.showing_partner(area)`

## The Game Plan, as machinery — what fires behind those four steps

Each promise is a real action with a real owner. Two of them are **gates**: the showing can't confirm until both
clear.

1. **Text the showing-agreement link** (AI → e-sign). Fires the e-sign to the buyer's phone the moment she's off
   the thread. Captures the agency relationship **before** the tour — the whole point of doing it here, not after.
2. **Confirm the window with the sellers** (AI → seller/TC · **GATE**). Our own listing → showing instructions +
   seller ping. Co-broke → the listing agent's showing system. **Nothing confirms to the buyer until this returns
   yes.** Declined → offer the nearest alternative.
3. **Both gates green → confirm the showing** (System · **GATE**). Agreement signed **and** seller confirmed →
   the green-light text with final time, address, what to bring. Unsigned at cutoff → one warm nudge.
4. **Warm-hand to the showing partner** (AI → partner). Brief the area partner (Trisha) with full context — who
   the buyer is, their story, the home, the time. She introduces herself and meets them at the house. The lead
   never feels handed off; they feel **upgraded** to a real person.

## Decision tree — where the conversation forks

She isn't on rails. Each fork changes the script or the machinery.

| Fork | Paths |
|---|---|
| **A · Do we have a phone number?** | Yes → full conversation over text · No → email-only variant of the same beats; capture a number early |
| **B · First-time vs experienced?** (beat 03) | First-time → lean into herd effect + lender help, more hand-holding · Experienced → shorter Curiosity, straight to logistics + agreement; respect their time |
| **C · Already working with another agent?** (beat 04) | No/solo → normal agreement framing ("the form that lets us show you") · Yes, committed → **do NOT poach**; gracious, flag to a human |
| **D · Seller confirms the requested time?** | Yes → green-light the buyer, proceed to handoff · No → offer the nearest workable window, re-confirm both sides |
| **E · Agreement signed before the showing?** | Signed → fully confirmed, partner briefed · Not yet → one warm nudge; still unsigned at cutoff → partner shows with a paper form (never lose the tour over paperwork) |
| **F · Does the reply need a human?** (anger, legal, price negotiation) | Routine → the AI handles · Out of scope → capture essentials, hand to owner/partner, tell the buyer a real person will follow up |

## The context map — every value, its source

| What she knows | Source | How she uses it |
|---|---|---|
| The home and the time | `hl_pixel_event` / FUB webhook | Opens on the exact ask; confirms the *what* instantly |
| Contact (name, phone, email) | `hl_leads` · `fub.people` | Personalizes; decides text vs email path |
| Every prior inquiry | `fub.events` | Infers active-shopper + price band + areas; fuels the reflection |
| The deep browse trail | `fub.zillow_home_activity` | Names the neighborhoods/homes they love → Curiosity that lands |
| Prior texts / calls / notes | `fub.text_messages` · `fub.calls` · `fub.notes` | Never repeats a question already answered; catches "working with an agent" |
| Lender / stage / tags | `fub.people` | Financing question framing; skip if already qualified |
| Seller side / showing rules | `Listing` · TC loop | The seller-confirm gate; our-listing vs co-broke path |
| Area showing partner | HL team roster (rotation) | Names Trisha in the Game Plan; briefs her for the handoff |
| Showing-agreement status | e-sign (Documenso) | The second gate; drives the nudge logic |

## The live-pilot hardening (2026-07-14) — what the real leads taught the framework

The 2026-07-14 live pilot (Ana's clone on real Zillow tour leads, driven by hand) added laws the original
architecture didn't carry. The full operating playbook — pull-in defaults, the first-touch sequence (card + one
message + Zillow link), the full message simulation, the close ladder, the Appointment Set workflow, and the
follow-up engine — lives in the **Closed Loop playbook** working artifact and lands here as it locks. The laws:

- **The Cold-Lead Law** — most buyers answer logistics instantly and ignore rapport questions. Questions must be
  cheap to answer (about the house/visit, five words to reply); an ignored question is DEAD (never re-ask); ONE
  question per text; match their temperature; collaboration must be EARNED (question → answer → one-line
  reflection before "here's the plan").
- **The Stale-Lead Nudge** — first touch + one hour of silence → exactly ONE nudge, ever: new substance from the
  dossier (prior agent era, their Zillow relationship), care frame not chase ("I'm the local agent assigned to
  your Zillow account and want to make sure you're taken care of"), never a repeat of the question.
- **The enforced sellers-wait** — confirmed times stay OUT of the AI's context until a human releases them; she
  cannot leak what she doesn't know.
- **One confirmation text, ever** — the close is one message; the machinery (calendar invite, task, partner
  brief) carries the rest silently.

## What's real today vs. what this needs

- **Live today — the data spine:** FUB history, the browse trail, the pixel tour-request signal, listing data,
  the AI's context builder.
- **Live today — the plumbing:** tour-request workflow, e-sign (Documenso), showing-partner roster, the
  iMessage/SMS send path.
- **To build — the conversation brain:** the state machine that runs Curiosity → Connection → Collaboration,
  reads each reply, and picks the branch. The scripts on this page + the pilot laws are its seed — being written
  now as the text prompt and the voice prompt.
- **To build — the two gates as one flow:** tie seller-confirm + agreement-signed into a single "showing
  confirmed" condition that green-lights the buyer and triggers the partner brief automatically.
