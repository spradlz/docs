HouseLoop is consolidating **all voice + SMS comms onto its own infrastructure** so every
call and text runs through the platform and **Myli** — not a third-party CRM. This page
covers how the business lines map to caller intent, and how inbound is captured.

<Note>
**The number a caller dials is intent data.** Each business line means something different,
so the inbound `To` number tells Myli *who is calling and why* before she answers — and she
opens with the right frame. The Twilio voice/SMS webhook reads the `To` number → looks up
that line's context → loads Myli's matching prompt.
</Note>

## Business lines → Myli context

Each owned line carries a distinct intent. The handler resolves context from the `To` number:

| Line role | Myli reads it as | Opening frame |
|---|---|---|
| **Company / Google My Business** (`913-320-0906`) | a new prospect who found us on Google | Qualify — buyer or seller? |
| **Listings / sign line** (on yard signs) | a caller standing at a specific listing | "Which property are you looking at?" — high intent, property-specific |
| **Legacy agent line** (a past agent's old number) | likely a **past client** of that agent | Warm; look them up in that agent's book |

The raw line inventory and per-number configuration live in the internal CRM settings, not
in these public docs. The routing model above is the contract: **`To` number → context → prompt.**

## Number ownership

HouseLoop owns its numbers in **Twilio** so they're portable and never hostage to a CRM
vendor. Numbers currently routed through a third party are re-pointed to the HouseLoop
voice/SMS webhooks; numbers provisioned inside another platform are **ported out to Twilio**
(standard carrier port — LOA + a recent bill, a few business days, no downtime).

<Tip>
A number provisioned *inside* a third-party CRM is owned by that CRM. Porting it to your own
Twilio account is what makes it yours — and lets you point its Voice + SMS webhooks at Myli.
</Tip>

Per-member dialer numbers, A2P 10DLC brand/campaign registration, and the per-user
caller-ID rules are documented in **[Team Members & Communications](/guides/team-communications)**.

## Inbound capture — every call and text lands on the lead

<Check>
**Live.** Inbound calls, texts, and emails are stored to the lead record and threaded onto
the conversation — **including callers who aren't a lead yet.**
</Check>

Inbound from an **unknown number** (a missed call, a voicemail, a first-touch text from
someone not yet in the system) is captured as an *unmatched* record keyed by phone number,
rather than dropped. This means the full inbox — missed calls and voicemails included — is
visible and actionable, and converts cleanly into a lead when the caller is identified.

| Inbound type | Captured | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Call** (incl. missed / voicemail) | ✅ | Recording + outcome stored; transcript when available |
| **Text** (SMS / iMessage) | ✅ | Threaded onto the lead; unknown-number texts captured too |
| **Email** | ✅ | Threaded onto the lead |
