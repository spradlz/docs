When a visitor signs up or signs in on `houseloop.ai`, two things happen: a **CRM lead** is
created, and — once we have a phone number — a **context-aware workflow** fires in Myli's voice.
This page covers the whole path: the front-end modal, the Google round-trip, the global phone
policy, exactly when the lead is created, the no-phone re-prompt loop, and the **New Lead (X)**
workflow family.

## The unified sign-in modal

Every auth/registration entry point opens **one** component — `components/site/SignInModal.tsx` —
so the experience is identical everywhere (like a home, save a search, the profile "Sign in"
button). It is fully tokenized (`HL_MODAL` tokens in `lib/tokens.ts`): one size, the HouseLoop
logo on **every** step, consistent verbiage.

It's a single-page "join or sign in" flow with an optional phone step:

| Step | What it asks | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `identify` | **Continue with Google** on top, then **or**, then an email field | One email field — we look it up and route to the right next screen |
| `details` | Password (email path only) | "Welcome back" for an existing email, "Create your account" for a new one |
| `profile` | **"You're almost there"** — first/last name + phone | The phone-capture step (governed by the global phone policy below) |

When the buyer types an email and hits **Continue with email**, a read-only lookup
(`GET /api/auth/email-exists`) decides whether they land on **Welcome back** (password → sign in)
or **Create your account** (password → phone). No manual "new vs returning" toggle.

## The auth gate — what's gated, and how it reads

`components/site/auth-gate.tsx` is the single choke point. A signed-out buyer who clicks a gated
action gets the modal with **contextual copy**; signed-in users pass straight through (unless they
still owe a phone — see the re-prompt loop below).

| Gate | Trigger | Headline style |
|---|---|---|
| `liked` / `saved` / `save-search` | a button **on a specific home** | "To save this home, **log in or create your free account**" |
| `all` / `updates` / `shared` | a **nav heading** (Favorites / Saved Searches / Shared) | "Sign in or create an account to see your…" |
| `generic` | the profile **Sign in** button | "Sign in" (no pill) |

`gateListingAction(type, status, hlid?)` is the helper every listing surface calls at the top of a
like/save handler — signed out → it opens the modal and stashes the pending like/save so it
**replays** after auth; signed in → the action proceeds. Sharing a home is **free / never gated**.

## Google return — back to the modal, not a white page

When the buyer picks **Continue with Google**, they leave to Google's native screen (expected).
The return lands them **back in the modal over the blurred page**, not on a standalone form:

```
Continue with Google
  → Google native screen
  → /auth/complete-profile router redirects to  <originalPage>?hlAuthProfile=1&ctx=<gateType>
  → AuthGateModal sees ?hlAuthProfile=1, re-opens SignInModal at the `profile` step
    ("You're almost there") over the blurred page, restoring the gate context from ?ctx=
```

`app/auth/complete-profile/page.tsx` is a pure **router** for the Google return (the old standalone
form is dead). It only bounces back with `?hlAuthProfile=1` when the global policy is **Required**
and the user has no phone yet; otherwise it sends them straight to where they were.

## The global phone policy (Required / Optional / Hidden)

One CRM setting controls how hard we push for a phone at registration:
**CRM → Settings → Website → Registration Settings → "Require phone number at registration"**
(`social_phone_requirement` in `hl_registration_settings`). The website reads it via
`GET /api/registration-settings` (cached 60s) and the modal honors it:

| Policy | Phone step | The X | No-phone outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Required** (PPC default) | shown, marked required | present (corner) | X creates an **email-only lead + "no phone" note** |
| **Optional** | shown, with a friendly **"Skip for now"** | present | skip creates an **email-only lead + "no phone" note** |
| **Hidden** | **not shown at all** | n/a | **email-only lead**, no note |

There is **always an X**, by design — you can never truly force a phone number (a trapped user
types a fake one or closes the tab). "Required" raises the friction (no friendly skip button, only
the corner X); it does not cage the buyer.

## When the lead is created — Finish or X, never at login

This is the core rule (set by Zach 2026-06-09). **Authenticating does NOT create a lead.** A
NextAuth `User` is just a login record. The CRM lead is created at the **resolution of the phone
step**, exactly two ways:

```
Google / email auth succeeds  →  NO CRM lead yet
   │
   ├─ Finish (enter phone)  →  lead created WITH phone  +  the gated like/save replays → HOME SAVED
   │
   └─ X / Skip              →  lead created (email + name) + "Registered without a phone number" note
                                 the gated like/save is CLEARED → the HOME IS NOT SAVED
```

- The single creation point is `complete-profile` → `pushLeadToCRM(...)` →
  `/api/integrations/website/lead-intake`. The two old "passive `account_created`" pushes at auth
  time (in `auth.ts` `events.createUser` and the `register` route) were **removed** — they created
  phone-less ghost leads and fired the "new lead" notification before the lead was real.
- **X / Skip** sends `phoneSkipped: true` → lead-intake adds the note, fires the
  `houseloop/registration.no-phone` signal (the update-phone nurture), and **suppresses the
  registration text workflow** (you can't text a numberless lead). **Hidden** sends
  `phoneHidden: true` → email-only lead, no note.
- Because **only Finish saves the home**, the phone is the carrot: give a number, keep the home.

## The no-phone re-prompt loop

A buyer who is **logged in but has no phone** (Required mode) does not get a free pass on the next
like/save. The engagement bar checks `needsPhone = phoneRequired && authenticated && !session.user.phone`
and, instead of saving, re-opens the phone step — **"Please enter your phone number to continue"**,
first/last name prefilled — every time, until they give a number:

```
logged in, no phone → click Like/Save
   → promptPhoneForAction(type, hlid)  (stashes the action, opens modal to the `profile` step)
        ├─ Finish → phone saved → the stashed like/save replays → HOME SAVED, loop ends
        └─ X      → home still not saved, back to normal — next save attempt re-prompts again
```

## Consent (TCPA)

The phone step carries a standardized **call/text consent disclaimer** —
`components/legal/ConsentFinePrint.tsx`, modeled on Zillow's. It's deliberately small, left-justified,
and sits directly under the phone field. Submitting the profile (Finish) is the consent action.

## The New Lead (X) family — entry-point routing

Each entry point creates a lead that fires **its own** workflow, named for *how* the person showed
up. The naming convention is **New Lead (X)**:

| Workflow | Fires when… | Event key |
|---|---|---|
| **New Lead (Registration only)** | bare signup, no other intent | `general_registration` |
| **New Lead (Liked Home)** | created an account by liking a home | `liked_home_registration` |
| **New Lead (Saved Home)** | created an account by saving a home | `saved_home_registration` |
| **New Lead (Tour Request)** | created an account booking a tour | `tour_requested` |
| **New Lead (Contact Request)** | created an account contacting an agent | `contact_requested` |

These live under **System Workflows → 🌐 Website — HouseLoop.ai → 📝 Registration** in the CRM,
*locked* (built, proven, then locked so they fire on every matching event for HouseLoop.ai leads).
The **X / no-phone** path does **not** fire these text-first workflows — it routes to the
update-phone nurture instead (see above).

### How the event reaches the workflow

The website posts the action to the CRM lead-intake, which upserts the lead, applies the matching
**event tag**, and fires the trigger:

```
website action  → /api/integrations/website/lead-intake
  → UPSERT hl_leads (by email), assign to Myli, put on Buyer pipeline @ New Lead
  → ACTION_TO_EVENT[action] = eventKey   (e.g. liked_home_registration)
  → apply the event tag  → emit houseloop/lead.tag-added   ← the generic workflow trigger
  → (+ EVENT_TO_INNGEST first-class event for built-in events)   ← suppressed when no phone
  → workflowDispatcher matches the lead source's event→workflow assignment and runs it
```

## High-intent flows always require a phone

Independent of the global policy, the four **high-intent** request forms hard-require a phone to
**submit** — Request a Tour, Contact an Agent, Get a Home Value Report, and Seller "Get Started."
Those are people who expect a call back, so the phone is mandatory on the form itself.

<Note>
**Upcoming — Hard-gated PPC forced registration (not yet built).** A planned PPC setting will let
you put a **hard wall** in front of the listings, beyond the soft phone capture above:

- **Register to view any home** — a logged-out visitor can't open a listing detail at all until
  they register, or
- **View N homes, then register** — let them browse a few (e.g. 3) listing details, then force
  registration to continue.

This builds on the existing `ppc_min_detail_views_for_registration` and
`ppc_photo_gating_enabled` settings (already in `hl_registration_settings`, not yet enforced as a
hard wall). It's a route/middleware gate on the listing-detail page, distinct from the in-modal
phone capture documented here. Tracking only — no behavior is live yet.
</Note>
