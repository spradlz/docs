## The three ways in (auth â€” `apps/houseloop-web/auth.ts`)

1. **"Continue with Google"** â€” `GoogleProvider` via PrismaAdapter.
2. **Email + password** â€” `CredentialsProvider` (bcrypt vs `User.passwordHash`; `/api/auth/register` creates the user + hash first).
3. **Magic token** â€” `verifyMagicToken` (`lib/auth/magic-token`): signed per-lead links in emails/agent
   flows auto-log the lead in (also how "Create Saved Search as Agent" opens the site AS the lead).

Account linking: `allowDangerousEmailAccountLinking = true` â€” email+password first, Google later (same
email) lands on the SAME User row, no duplicates. Sessions are JWT (hydrated with `id` + `phone`).

## The Finish/X phone law

A lead is created at **registration completion**, never at login, in exactly two shapes
(the phone-capture step after account creation, governed by the `social_phone_requirement` admin setting):

- **Finish** â€” the visitor completes the phone step â†’ lead created WITH phone.
- **X (skip)** â€” the visitor closes the phone step â†’ lead created with **no phone + a "no phone" note**
  (`phoneSkipped`). No hidden nurture fires (the old orphan `registration.no-phone` event was deleted
  2026-07-06 â€” a phone-chase belongs on the workflow canvas).

Registration UX toggles (phone required/optional/hidden, international, required-field asterisks, and
`phone_required_on_showing_request` â€” enforced on the tour route since 2026-07-06) live in the CRM at
**Settings â†’ Website â†’ Registration Settings**, read by the site via
`GET /api/integrations/website/registration-settings` (public read-only, 60s cache; PUT self-gates).

## The intake contract â€” `ACTION_TO_EVENT`

Every site action that should touch the CRM POSTs to **`/api/integrations/website/lead-intake`** (CRM),
authenticated by the per-source `x-houseloop-intake-secret` header. The payload's `action` maps through
the **`ACTION_TO_EVENT`** table to ONE event key â€” the event fires the door workflow, stamps the tag,
and drives everything downstream. Highlights (full map in the route):

| `action` | event |
|---|---|
| `showing_request` / `contact_agent` | `tour_request` / `contact_request` |
| `seller_request` / `claim` / `seller_inquiry` | `home_value_report` (merged â€” /sell Get Report AND the listing "Is this your home?") |
| `seller_get_started` | `seller_get_started` |
| `saved_search` / `_agent` / `_system` | three FIRST-CLASS triggers by ORIGIN (client vs agent-for-lead vs system-seed) |
| `google_signin` | `general_registration` |
| `liked/saved/shared_home_registration` | per-entry-point registration doors |

**An unmapped action creates the lead with NO event** (warn-logged: "add it to ACTION_TO_EVENT or fix
the caller") â€” no tag, no workflow. The `REG_VARIANT` map splits new-lead registration doors from
existing-lead acks by the `created` flag.

## The shared-home gate

Sharing a home is a **login-gated registration event** (Zach 2026-07-01): the share modal opens for
everyone, the gate fires at SEND â€” a signed-out sharer registers first (`shared_home_registration`
door), and the recipient flows through lead-intake too.

## The pixel identity stitch

The first-party pixel (`lib/pixel/hl-pixel.ts` â†’ CRM `/api/pixel/track`) tracks anonymous browsing;
at registration/login the visitor's anonymous events are stitched to the lead so pre-registration
views/likes appear on their CRM timeline (and power the pixel-derived starter saved search, seeded
`createdBy='system'`).
