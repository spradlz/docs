A CRM can't naively forbid two leads from sharing a phone number — shared numbers
are legitimate (spouses, a business line, a parent texting on a kid's behalf). But
an *undetected* shared number is dangerous here, because the [AI Clone](/guides/ai-clone)
routes inbound texts to a lead **by phone number**. This guide covers the rules and
the detection that keeps that safe.

## Lead-creation rules

When a lead is created (manual **+ Add New Lead** modal, or the website
`/api/integrations/website/lead-intake`):

| Field | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Email** | **Hard-unique.** `hl_leads` has a `uq_hl_leads_email` index. A second lead with the same email is rejected (`POST /api/leads` → `409`). |
| **Phone** | **Allowed to repeat, but detected.** Creating a lead with a phone that already exists triggers a *soft warning* (below), not a hard block. |

## How inbound texts find their lead (phone routing)

Every inbound text resolves which lead it belongs to **purely by phone number**.
The iMessage webhook (`app/api/bb/inbound`) and the Twilio SMS webhook
(`app/api/phone/webhooks/sms`) both:

1. Take the sender's number, strip everything but digits, keep the **last 10**.
2. Match it against `hl_leads.phone` (same last-10 normalization), `deleted_at IS NULL`.
3. `ORDER BY created_at DESC LIMIT 1` — **the newest matching lead wins.**

That last step is a silent tiebreaker: if two active leads carry the same number,
the **older one never receives the text** and the AI clone never sees it. Today
that's harmless (numbers are unique in practice), but nothing structurally prevents
a collision — which is why we detect it.

<Note>
The duplicate detector uses the **same** last-10-digit normalization
(`phoneTail10` in `lib/leads/duplicates.ts`) as the inbound webhooks, so the
banner's idea of "duplicate" always matches who the clone actually routes to.
</Note>

## Soft duplicate-phone warning (on create)

`POST /api/leads` checks for an existing active lead with the same phone **before**
inserting:

- If a match exists and the request didn't pass `force: true` → it returns
  **`409 { code: "duplicate_phone", duplicates: [...] }`** instead of creating.
- The **+ Add New Lead** modal catches that response and shows the matched leads
  (name + link to each) with a **"Create anyway"** button.
- "Create anyway" re-submits with `force: true`, which bypasses the check and
  creates the lead. The choice stays with the human — we warn, we don't block.

The email path is unaffected — a duplicate email is still hard-blocked by the
unique index in the insert's catch.

## Collision banner (on lead detail)

The lead detail page renders a `DuplicateLeadsBanner` that calls
`GET /api/leads/duplicates?leadId=<id>`. When this lead's phone (or email) is
shared with other active leads, it shows an amber bar listing them, each linking
to its detail page, and notes that **inbound texts route to the most recently
created one**. When the lead is clean (the common case) the banner renders nothing.

## The detector API

`GET /api/leads/duplicates` — backed by `lib/leads/duplicates.ts`:

| Query | Use |
|---|---|
| `?leadId=<uuid>` | Duplicates of an existing lead (the detail banner). |
| `?phone=…&email=…&excludeLeadId=…` | Pre-create check (the Add Lead form). |

Returns `{ ok, duplicates: [{ id, name, phone, email, source, stage, created_at, match }] }`
where `match` is `phone`, `email`, or `both`. Matching is phone last-10 **OR**
case-insensitive email, newest first.

## Roadmap

- **Merge Leads** — pick a survivor and combine two leads (fields, messages,
  appointments, notes, stages, tags), soft-deleting the loser. The detection here
  is the surface that feeds it.
- **Smarter routing tiebreaker** — route an inbound text to the *most recently
  active* (or assigned) lead instead of the newest-created, and flag genuinely
  ambiguous numbers.
