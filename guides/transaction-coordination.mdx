## What this section is

**Transaction Coordination** is how HouseLoop connects to the systems where a deal actually
lives after it goes under contract — the transaction-management platforms that hold the loop /
transaction file: participants, documents, signatures, milestones, and close dates.

Where [Website Integration](/guides/listing-lifecycle) is about the *listing* (the home, its
data, its public page) and the CRM guides are about the *lead* (the person and their activity),
**Transaction Coordination is about the deal** — the contract-to-close record.

The first integration in this section is **[dotloop](/guides/dotloop-integration)**, and it is
the foundation the rest of the section builds on.

## The model

| Layer | Source of truth | HouseLoop role |
|---|---|---|
| **Listing** (the home) | `Listing` (web DB) | renders `/home/<slug>`, value, history |
| **Lead** (the person) | `hl_leads` (CRM DB) | CRM, AI follow-up, activity log |
| **Deal** (the transaction) | the transaction platform (dotloop) | **pulled in via this section** |

A transaction platform owns the contract-to-close file. We read it through the platform's API and
link each loop back to the HouseLoop lead/deal it belongs to — we do not replace the platform, and
we never move the brokerage's transaction data out of it. The platform stays the system of record;
HouseLoop reads from it.
