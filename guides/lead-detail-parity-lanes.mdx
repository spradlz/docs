The lead detail page has **two parity lanes** that render the exact same skeleton:

- **LEFT lane** — the **human agent + their AI clone** (the human's own AI, acting *as* the human).
- **RIGHT lane** — **Myli**, the assigned AI (relationship owner).

Both lanes can be active at once — LEFT (clone) and RIGHT (Myli) are two distinct AI actors, never
merged into "one AI responder". See [AI Clone](/guides/ai-clone) for clone-vs-Myli identity.

## One skeleton, both lanes

Each lane stacks the same two sections, top to bottom:

```
┌ LANE (LEFT = human+clone · RIGHT = Myli) ─────────────┐
│ 📋 TASKS & APPOINTMENTS        ← ALWAYS visible        │  dynamic items OUTSIDE a workflow
│    • appointments (future)                             │  hl_appointments (assignee = this lane's user)
│    • tasks (open)                                      │  hl_tasks       (assignee = this lane's user)
│ ━━ 🤖 AI WORKFLOW ━━           ← gated                 │  divider + heading
│    Active Triggers                                     │
│    Current Workflow                                    │
│    Current Action                                      │
│    Next Actions                                        │  the workflow chain + AI-owned mirrored items
│    Recent Actions                                      │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 📋 Tasks & Appointments — always on

This section is the **canonical home** for a lead's dynamic tasks and appointments — the items that
live *outside* a workflow. It is **never gated**: it shows whether AI mode is on or off, on both
lanes. Each lane shows only the tasks/appointments whose assignee resolves to that lane's user.

- Open tasks (`hl_tasks`) assigned to this lane's user.
- Future appointments (`hl_appointments`) assigned to this lane's user.

### 🤖 AI Workflow — gated

Below a `🤖` divider sits the AI reasoning chain, in the exact order operations happen:

| Section | Answers | Fed by |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Active Triggers** | What happened? | `hl_workflow_runs.trigger_payload` |
| **Current Workflow** | What's running? | `hl_workflows` via `hl_leads.active_workflow_id` |
| **Current Action** | What's it doing right now? | `hl_workflow_runs.current_node_id` |
| **Next Actions** | What happens next? | the workflow chain **+** AI-owned mirrored items |
| **Recent Actions** | What already happened? | `hl_lead_activity` (`actor_role='ai_agent'`) |

The AI Workflow section is what the `aiActive` gate controls:

- **RIGHT (Myli)** shows the AI Workflow whenever Myli is engaged on the lead.
- **LEFT (clone)** shows it when the human's **AI Mode** is on.

The gate now covers **only** the AI Workflow section — never Tasks & Appointments.

<Note>
Both lanes share ONE component (`AiReasoningChain`), driven by an `actor` param (`clone` | `myli`).
Change one, you change both — the two lanes are literally identical in structure.
</Note>

## Next Actions vs Tasks & Appointments (the rule)

This is the distinction that removes the old conflation:

- **Next Actions** = the running chain of a **workflow** (workflow-derived steps) **plus** any
  task/appointment an **AI** owns, **mirrored up**. A mirror shows in *both* places; its home in
  Tasks & Appointments stays put.
- **Human-owned** tasks/appointments live **only** in Tasks & Appointments — they never appear in
  Next Actions.

So `hl_tasks` / `hl_appointments` come **out** of the Next-Actions union; Next Actions keeps
`hl_scheduled_actions` (the workflow/AI chain) plus a mirror of AI-assigned tasks/appointments.

When a clone owns an item, it renders **faint in Tasks & Appointments** (the AI has it) and **lit in
Next Actions** (the AI is running it).

## Why this exists

The LEFT lane's enriched column was gated behind `ai_mode_enabled && humanOwned`. Because "Next
Actions" was a union of `hl_scheduled_actions` + `hl_tasks` + `hl_appointments`, that gate also hid
the human's own tasks/appointments when AI mode was off — the column showed on old AI-on leads and
vanished on newer ones, which read as a regression. Splitting Tasks & Appointments into its own
always-on section, and gating only the AI Workflow, fixes it cleanly on both lanes.

<Note>
**Status.** Tasks & Appointments as its own always-on section on both lanes, and mirroring AI-owned
items into Next Actions, are the intended behavior in the parity build; today the sections are still
being split out of the single gated column. The parity model and the mirror rule are the SSOT for
that build.
</Note>
