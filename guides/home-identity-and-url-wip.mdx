## The thesis (LOCKED — Zach 2026-06-27)
**The rooftop is the identity.** Every house gets ONE minted, frozen **`HLID`** pinned to its **coordinate + unit** —
not `md5(address)`. The URL is permanent (never re-indexes) = the local-SEO moat. Address/ZIP/MLS#/status are mutable
attributes; the HLID and the URL never move. (`HLP-` is the old prefix being renamed to `HLID-`; grandfathered strings
stay frozen.)

## The measured reality (2026-06-27 PM — web DB, centerbeam)
- **Homes are already hydrated and already carry an id.** 355,599 of 355,694 (**100.0%**) have an `hlPropertyId`;
  only **95** lack one (27 of them live). **99.9%** carry coordinates. So the job is **standardize → freeze → de-fork**
  the id, NOT a from-scratch mint.
- **Real same-home fork count = 2,163** (unit-aware). The earlier "~75" was only the *ZIP-differ* subset; the 2,957 raw
  count was inflated by ~794 multi-unit buildings. Forks are mostly **dropped directionals** (`928 NE 87th` vs `928
  87th`), **suffix variants**, and **ZIP drift** — at the same rooftop.

## ⚠️ The hard part (Zach called it): UNIT + COORDINATES
A naive "collapse same-rooftop → one id" would have been a **disaster**. Measured:
- **Only 1.1% of homes carry a unit** (4,003 / 355,694) — the `unit` column is almost entirely empty.
- **19,307 rows sit on a rooftop shared by 2+ distinct homes** (condos/duplexes/apartments); **only 14.3% carry a
  unit** → **16,538 rows on shared rooftops have NO unit.**
- Coord-pin alone collapses ~**52%** of the 2,163 forks (forked slugs share coords within ~25 m, e.g. `511 Callie` ≡
  `511 S Callie`); the other ~48% have coords >25 m apart (geocoder variance on the same home).

**Therefore the resolver design (the safe one):**
1. **Identity = normalized address (street + unit) PRIMARY; the coordinate CONFIRMS, it never merges on its own.**
   Coord-pin only collapses two rows when they're the *same address*, not merely the same rooftop.
2. **Reconstruct the unit** — ~99% of real units are embedded in the street string (`4545 Wornall St #208`), not the
   `unit` column. Parse those out so multi-unit buildings key correctly.
3. **Hard over-merge guard:** never fuse two unit-less rows that share a rooftop. Under-merge (a leftover fork) is
   recoverable; over-merge (fusing two people's condos into one HLID) is not.
4. **Street rung backstop:** normalize directionals/suffixes so `Callie` ≡ `S Callie`, `179th` ≡ `W 179th` mint the
   same HLID even when the geocoder disagrees.
