## The thesis (one line)
**The rooftop is the identity.** Every house gets ONE minted, frozen HouseLoop ID pinned to its lat/lng — not
`md5(address)`. The home URL is permanent (never re-indexes), which is the entire local-SEO moat. Address, ZIP,
MLS#, status are mutable attributes; the id and the URL never move.

## Why (the bug)
`hlPropertyId = "HLP-" + md5(identityKey)` where `identityKey = canonical street + unit + ZIP5`. The id **is** the
address, hashed — and ZIP is in it. Sources disagree on a home's ZIP (Zillow `411 NW 71st Ter, Kansas City, 64119`
vs MLS `411 NW 71st Terrace, Gladstone, 64118` — the same house), so the home hashes to two ids → two slugs → two
pages. **~75 such forks are live** (each a duplicate-content pair splitting Google authority). Google barely indexes
the site, compounded by **relative canonical tags** (no `metadataBase`).

## Architecture truth (measured)
- The **live URL identity = `Listing.canonicalSlug`** (`/home/[slug]` resolves by it). `hl_property`/`hlPropertyId`
  is a **side table** not wired to any URL/render path → low blast radius to fix.
- One `/home/<canonicalSlug>` per home already shipped (2026-06-18 URL collapse); `/listings/*` 301s in; the route
  301s a variant slug → the stored canonical. We **reuse** this — no new 301 campaign.
- 100% of listings carry coordinates (free from Repliers) → coordinates are the drift-proof spine.
