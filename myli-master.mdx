## The no-response cascade (proven 2026-07-06, gated off)

Myli is never idle — after every touch she leaves a **floor**: one scheduled future reach-out. How fast that floor comes back is driven by the lead's **stage**, and the aggressive "chase a new lead" behavior is folded into the same one scheduler — it is NOT a separate engine:

- A brand-new lead (**New Lead** / **Attempted Contact**) gets a **daily** touch.
- After **4 un-answered** touches in a row, Myli **auto-moves them to Nurture Warm**, which reprices the floor to **every 2 weeks**. Keep going quiet → she keeps easing off (Warm → Cold → monthly).
- The moment the lead **replies**, the streak resets to zero and she re-engages.

Everything is visible and editable: the cadence + the "after N attempts, move to X stage" rule live in one config row (`hl_settings.ai_closed_loop.cadence`), and every auto-move writes an audited stage-change row you can see on the lead. Proven end-to-end on a test lead (under-cap stays daily · at-cap moves to Warm + reprices · a reply resets the streak). Shipped dormant — nothing fires on real leads until the master flag is flipped.

## The two-beat registration model (LAW 1 — shipped on all 11 registration doors, proven 2026-07-06)

Every registration or inquiry now sends **two beats**, in order:

- **Beat 1 — the brand ack.** An instant, agent-agnostic "HouseLoop Team, we got your request" email. No personal name, no personal number, no signature — it's from the brand. This is the receipt.
- **Beat 2 — the personal beat.** Myli's own message (or your clone's, if configured) that actually moves things forward and books directly. This is the only beat that's allowed to be personal.

It's built the auditable way: Beat 1 is a **visible first step on each intent canvas** (brand ack → the New/Existing branch → Myli's personal beat), reusing the brand templates that already exist — nothing hidden, nothing hardcoded. **Live-proven**: a real tour request fired the brand `tour_confirmation` first, then Myli's personal `beta_tour_request` email, and the run completed clean.

Shipped on **all 11 registration/inquiry doors** — tour, contact, liked, saved, saved-search, shared, general registration, seller get-started, home-value report, property inquiry, and rental inquiry. Two batches, both live-proven (tour request fired the brand ack + Myli's personal beat; general registration fired its brand ack, run completed). Pure behavior events (viewed a home, virtually toured) don't get a brand ack — there's no "request" to acknowledge.

## The follow-up engine — the full end-to-end build (2026-07-06)

Everything below shipped in one pass. All of it is **gated OFF for real leads** (config flags / test-lead scope) and proven on the controlled test lead. It's the machinery that makes Myli own the relationship from first touch to booked showing and beyond.

**1. The never-idle floor + the no-response cascade.** After any touch, Myli leaves a scheduled next touch ("the floor"), paced by the lead's stage. A brand-new lead who goes quiet gets a daily nudge; after 4 unanswered touches she auto-moves them to Nurture Warm, which slows her to every 2 weeks — the aggressive chase and the gentle nurture are *one* stage-driven dial you can see and edit, and every auto-move is an audited line on the lead. Proven: under-cap stays daily, at-cap moves + reprices, a reply resets the counter.

**2. The daily hand-curated home.** For an actively-shopping lead (with no pending showing), Myli sends one hand-picked active home per day, tied to their saved search, deduped so she never repeats one, framed as her own find (never "I saw you browsing"). It respects the pause — nothing goes out while a showing is booked. Proven across all its gates.

**3. The objection → saved-search funnel.** When a lead pushes back or opens up about what they really want (a FORD moment — family, job, hobbies, dreams), Myli distills it into criteria and spins up a standing saved search, so matches keep flowing even after a "no." New tool (`create_saved_search`), a real web-DB search, framed as her keeping a lookout. Proven end-to-end. On registration a starter search is already auto-seeded from what they browsed.

**4. The pause rule.** A booked-but-not-yet-happened showing suppresses every hijack — the behavioral triggers and the aggressive floor go quiet until it's over; only the post-showing check-in fires. One active thing at a time; a booked showing wins.

**5. Who reaches out × who gets the appointment.** Per-source config decides the reacher-outer (Myli by default, or an agent whose AI clone handles it) and the appointment handoff — a HouseLoop agent *or* an external showing partner like Trisha who isn't a CRM user at all (she gets texted the showing; Myli stays the coordinator). Resolves through the source → platform hierarchy, never hardcoded.

**6. Every inbound door verified.** The full payload matrix fired every registration/inquiry door on the test lead and confirmed each runs + sends its brand ack. It also caught a real gap — three doors (property inquiry, rental inquiry, virtually toured) had live canvases but weren't registered to receive their events — now fixed.

## The e-sign closed loop (built 2026-07-06, e2e with Benji pending deploy)

When Myli sends an agreement for e-signature (Benji's e-sign plumbing), the conversational loop is now hers end to end:

1. **She texts the link.** The `document_sent` door runs a visible one-step canvas — Myli texts the lead "here's the {agreement} to sign so we can get you in to see the home, sign here: {link}." The send resolves AS the acting agent (Myli / Zach's clone / Ana's clone), never hardcoded.
2. **She chases if it goes unsigned.** A scheduled nudge is queued, timed to the showing (the day before). At fire time it re-reads the document — it only nudges if the doc is still out and unsigned, and cancels itself the moment they sign (belt-and-suspenders with Benji's webhook).
3. **She confirms on signature.** The `document_signed` door runs a second canvas — "got your signature, you're all set!"

Both messages are visible canvas steps; the nudge is the same scheduled-action engine as the floor. Along the way this surfaced and fixed a real gap — the workflow text couldn't see a door's own payload fields (so the signing link rendered blank), now fixed so any door field is addressable in the canvas. Final end-to-end proof runs with Benji firing a fresh document.
