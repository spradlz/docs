This week we rebuilt the listing detail page with five new data-driven sections so buyers get richer context on every home.

## New features

### Offer Insights v3

Every listing now shows a four-band offer-strength widget — Strong, Solid, Fair, or Weak — plus a plain-English read on the probability of winning the home at list price. Bands are derived from recent sold comps using a neighborhood → ZIP → city → county fallback, so you get the most local signal available. A "Compare nearby" link sends you straight to the matching market report.

Learn more in the [Listing Detail Page guide](/guides/listing-detail-page).

### Market Estimate v3

A new Market Estimate block shows the local median sold price and how the listing's asking price compares (`+X%` / `-X%`). Prices render in full (`$1,160,000`), and the deep-link jumps to the local market trends section for fast comparison.

### Tax card

Listings now display the most recent annual property tax amount sourced from the MLS. Listings without tax data omit the card cleanly — no placeholders. Coverage starts at launch and grows toward ~80%+ as the backfill completes.

### Schools card

Each listing surfaces its school district plus assigned elementary, middle, and high schools when available. Launch coverage is ~46% and will grow with the catalog backfill.

### HOA / Community card

Subdivision listings now show community amenities — pool, tennis, clubhouse, gated, exercise room, walking trails — alongside HOA fee and frequency when reported. Data updates nightly so there's no extra load on page render.

## Updates

### Mobile section order

On mobile, Offer Insights and Market Estimate now appear directly after the About section so buyers see market context before scrolling through feature lists.

### Pill labels tuned for mobile

Offer Insights pills use short labels (Strong / Solid / Fair / Weak) on small screens to prevent truncation, while the longer semantic labels remain available on desktop.

## Removed

### KCRAR market snapshot retired

The legacy KCRAR market snapshot is no longer used on the listing detail page. All market context — bands, medians, and sell-to-list — now flows from the unified daily and nightly geo-market datasets, giving more consistent numbers across listing pages, market reports, and search.
