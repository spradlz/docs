##### THE PROBLEM
Past-client homes come from **typed source data** (old CRM/SISU entries), so the street is frequently garbled ‚Äî a
wrong house number, a misspelled or abbreviated street, or a **missing city**. A missing city is the worst: CASS
then geocodes the fragment to the **wrong state entirely** (`105 E Washington` with no city ‚Üí Attica, **Indiana**).
Address text alone cannot be trusted, so the address-only reconciliation engine (identityKey ‚Üí LLM ‚Üí Redfin) can
land on the wrong rooftop or mint a fork.

##### THE CORE INSIGHT ‚Äî the MLS# is the ground-truth anchor
You **cannot search** Zillow by MLS number, but Zillow's **property-details** page carries it
(`attributionInfo.mlsId`). So the resolver treats the MLS# as truth and the typed address as a hint:
resolve an address ‚Üí get the `zpid` ‚Üí read `property-details(zpid).attributionInfo.mlsId` ‚Üí **validate it against
our known MLS#(s)** for the home = 100% certainty it's the right rooftop, not "probably."

   (CASS mangles rural/garbled streets) ‚Üí street-type variants (St/Ter/Dr/Ct/Ln) ‚Üí range-preserved
   (`10700-10702`) ‚Üí original. Pick the candidate by **house# + street-name-token overlap**. Multi-unit buildings:
   pick the unit from Zillow's autocomplete **dropdown** ‚Üí the unit's own `zpid` (a location string with `APT n`
   won't match).
3. **Validate via MLS#** ‚Äî `property-details(zpid).attributionInfo.mlsId` vs our known MLS#s

4. **Classify (never silently wrong):** `confirmed` (MLS-locked) ¬∑ `address_match` (house#+street verified, Zillow
   exposed no mlsId ‚Äî normal for older sales) ¬∑ `mls_mismatch` (flag) ¬∑ `not_found` (flag).
