**What this is.** A repeatable way to produce the exact deliverable we built for the Lawrence Gunther

</Note>

## The deliverable

One merged, full-resolution **US-Letter print PDF** (`Spradling-Group-Listing-Presentation-<name>.pdf`):

- **Pages 1–17 — the listing presentation.** Cover (prepared for the seller, with their address), our
  story, marketing, the Ten Steps to Sold, seller reviews on every page, and the "Thank You / Your
  Questions" close.
- **Pages 18–33 — the CMA.** Cover, Executive Summary (the Min / **Recommended** / Max price graphic
  over the subject's stat cards), one page per comparable (with a **difference column** and a plain-English
  bottom line), and a full MLS data sheet per comp (each led by that property's hero photo).

Every page is a discrete letter sheet — verified by rendering, not assumed.

## How to talk to Benji

You do not need to walk Benji through tools step by step. Say what you want; he runs the deterministic
build. The two things he needs are an **address** and the **CMA comp data** (see the two halves below).

<Steps>
<Step title="Kick it off">
"Benji, build a listing presentation for **&lt;address&gt;**, prepared for **&lt;seller name&gt;**."
If you already have the CMA HTML (from the MLS pull), point him at it. If not, that's the one step that
needs a live MLS session — see [The two halves](#the-two-halves).
</Step>

<Step title="Review the on-screen version">
Benji publishes each half as an artifact you can open and read on screen, and renders individual pages
to confirm formatting. Nothing goes to print unseen.
</Step>

<Step title="Iterate in plain English">
Talk to him like an editor. Real examples from this build:
- "The Google reviews are tiny — make them bigger and fill the blank space."
- "Even up the Your Home and Comparable columns; the difference column can stay small."
- "Add a difference column: green if we're ahead, yellow if not — never red."
- "Re-look at the price. $875K doesn't make sense against the $825K pending twin — take a second pass."
- "Move the price page into the Executive Summary and merge it with the stat cards."

Each request is a real edit to the source, re-rendered and re-verified — not a one-off.
</Step>

<Step title="Ship the print PDF">
When it's right, Benji merges the presentation and CMA into one letter-size PDF on the Desktop and
confirms the page count and that every page fits. That file goes to the printer.
</Step>

<Step title="Send it to the print shop (optional)">
Benji can email the printer directly (compose in Gmail, attach the files, hold for your sign-off,
send, then confirm delivery and watch for a bounce). Two hard rules: the print-shop email address is
**verified, never guessed** (we bounced once on a made-up domain), and Benji **never clicks Send until
you confirm.** For a shop that trims, he runs the bleed + crop-marks pass first.
</Step>
</Steps>

## The two halves

The workflow has exactly one non-deterministic step. Knowing which half you're in explains every
"reproducible vs. judgment" question.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
<Card title="1. Data (the MLS pull)" icon="database">
Auth-gated and semi-manual — a skill file cannot log into the MLS. Runs inside an **already-authenticated
Heartland MLS Matrix session**: pick subdivision-anchored comps, capture full data sheets, extract photos.
Produces a self-contained CMA HTML. This is the companion **CMA data-pull skill**
(`SKILL-cma-data-pull.md` in the toolkit). It is the only part that needs a live browser.
</Card>
<Card title="2. Build (the brand + merge)" icon="wand-magic-sparkles">
100% deterministic — it's **code, not vibe**. Takes the CMA data + subject facts and produces the
branded, merged print PDF. This is what makes the deliverable reproducible every time. See
[The Reproducible Build Engine](/listing-presentation/build-engine).
</Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
**Why it won't "fail to connect."** The part that *can* fail — logging into the MLS — is isolated in
half 1, and when a session is dead the skill's rule is to **stop and tell you**, not fake data. Half 2
never touches the network or a login; it's local scripts, so it produces the same result every run.
</Note>

## What's reproducible vs. what's judgment

Being honest about this is the whole point — it's why the output is trustworthy.

| Reproducible (the engine does it) | Per-listing judgment (Benji reasons through it) |
| --- | --- |
| Rebrand to navy + blue, our lockup, tokens | Which comps are the floor / ceiling / context |
| The difference column math + colors | The **recommended price** and the "why this range" story |
| Hero-photo-per-sheet, even columns, merge | Reconciling contradictions (e.g. a pending twin priced below ours) |
| Letter-size pagination + print verification | What counts as an honest caveat to state out loud |

The pricing analysis is **not** automated, and shouldn't be — it's a real comparative read of the comps.
The engine guarantees the *look and structure* are identical every time; Benji guarantees the *analysis*
is honest for that specific home.

<Warning>

because we used `mailcopyplus.com`; the real domain is `mailandcopyplus.com` (note the **and**). Confirm the address
from a real listing (their FedEx/UPS/Facebook page) or ask, then send. After sending, check

</Warning>

**The order we placed** (two jobs, handled differently in one email):

- **Job 1 — the listing presentation:** print in **full color** and **bind** (one bound copy).
- **Job 2 — the seller paperwork** (Listing Docs for Signature, Sellers Estimated Proceeds Worksheet,
  Ownership / Tax Datasheet, Sellers Disclosures, Previous MLS Sheet): print each and **staple each
  document individually** — every PDF's pages stapled into its own set, and the sets **not** stapled
  to each other. Five separate stapled stacks.
- Pickup time and the agent picking up go in the subject + body; a cell number for questions goes in
  the body.
