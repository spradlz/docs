**In the app:** CRM sidebar ‚Üí **User Settings** ‚Üí **Profile Settings** ‚Äî [`/settings/profile`](https://crm.houseloop.ai/settings/profile)

<Info>There is no standalone profile form. `/settings/profile` immediately resolves the signed-in user and redirects to that user's row in the shared member editor at `/settings/team/agents/[id]` ‚Äî the same page used to edit any teammate. "My Profile" / "Account" and "edit a member" are one editor, self or others. The old dedicated profile form has been retired.</Info>

## What it does

`/settings/profile` is **not a standalone form** ‚Äî it is a thin redirect. On load it calls `GET /api/profile` to find the signed-in user's id, then `router.replace`s to that user's row in the shared member editor at **`/settings/team/agents/{id}`**. If the id can't be resolved it falls back to `/settings/team`. While resolving it shows an "Opening your profile‚Ä¶" placeholder.

The member editor is one scrollable page with a filter bar ‚Äî `All ¬∑ Permissions ¬∑ Profile ¬∑ Connections ¬∑ Roles` ‚Äî plus an `AI Clone` tab for human members. Edits auto-save (debounced ~600ms after the last change) and a **Save** button forces an immediate save.

## What you configure

The identity fields live in the member editor's **Profile** section:

| Field | Editable | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Name | Yes | `full_name`. Re-issues the session cookie so the sidebar name updates immediately. |
| Email | No | Read-only sign-in identity, managed via Google. |
| Phone | Yes | `phone`. |
| Time zone | Yes | `timezone` (Eastern / Central / Mountain / Pacific). |

The surrounding editor also exposes (subject to permissions), documented in full with the Team / member editor:

- **Contact Cards** ‚Äî the member's **vCard** (savable contact) and **Digital Business Card** (branded image). See below.
- **Permissions** ‚Äî Access level (Owner / Admin / Member) and member Type (Human / AI Agent). Only admins can change another member's access, and you cannot change your **own** access.
- **Connections** ‚Äî Email, Calendar, Phone, and iMessage channel connections.
- **Roles** ‚Äî job roles for humans; AI-kind roles with per-role persona/tool/doc overrides for AI agents.
- **AI Clone** ‚Äî human members only: assign and configure the AI clone that sends as you (your phone, email, calendar).

## Contact Cards (vCard + Digital Business Card)

Each human member (and Myli, the standalone AI agent) has **two** shareable cards, edited in two sections of the member editor. Both are **data-driven** ‚Äî every field lives in `hl_users.data`, nothing is hardcoded ‚Äî and both are **public** so a text message can attach them (Twilio MMS / iMessage fetch them unauthenticated, the same mechanism Follow Up Boss uses).

<Info>These are two different things. The **vCard** is a real savable contact (`.vcf`) ‚Äî the recipient taps it and gets the native "Add Contact" screen. The **Digital Business Card** is a branded **image** (`.png`) you text as a picture ‚Äî no save button. Per-human **AI clones** never get cards; standalone agents (Myli) do.</Info>

### vCard

The savable contact. Editable fields: **Name** (`full_name`), **Title** (`data.title`), **Mobile** (`data.card_phone` ‚Äî a dropdown of the member's own assigned lines: Twilio dialer / iMessage / cell), **Email** (`data.card_email` ‚Äî a dropdown of known addresses or a custom one), **Homepage** (`data.card_homepage`, default `houseloop.ai`), and **Notes** (`data.card_note`, default `Spradling Group powered by HouseLoop`).

The section shows a live preview of what the card looks like when sent ‚Äî the **collapsed** suggestion row and the **expanded** iOS contact card ‚Äî plus a Copy-link and Download-`.vcf` control.

Card construction rules (learned on real devices):

- The **header is name + photo only** ‚Äî no `ORG`, no company mash-up. iOS combines title + company into the line above the name and it reads cluttered, so the brand lives in **Notes** instead. If **both** title and company are blank, iOS shows "Unknown" there, so a title (e.g. "Realtor") is what fills that slot.
- The **headshot is embedded as base64** inside the `.vcf`. iOS refuses to fetch a remote `PHOTO;VALUE=URI`, so a linked photo shows initials ‚Äî the bytes must be inline.
- Served as `text/vcard` at **`/api/team/vcard/[id]`** (public). A shareable web version lives at **`/c/[id]`** (a clean contact page with a "Save to Contacts" button).

### Digital Business Card

A branded card **image** you text as a picture. Editable fields: **Name**, **Title**, **Mobile**, **Email** (same data as the vCard ‚Äî an edit syncs both). Rendered server-side (Satori / `next/og`) as a PNG at **`/api/team/card-image/[id]`** (public). The preview in the editor **is** that exact PNG shown large, so it always matches what gets sent.

### Sending them

The lead text composer gets two separate controls ‚Äî **Send vCard** and **Send Business Card** ‚Äî that attach the respective artifact (SMS via Twilio `MediaUrl`, iMessage via a BlueBubbles attachment). The picker list comes from **`/api/team/cards`**.

<Tip>A member's headshot lives in the website app at `houseloop.ai/brand/headshots/<name>.jpg` and must be `https` or the card falls back to initials. `tools/houseloop-cards/enhance-headshot.mjs` (Gemini "nano-banana") can face-restore/upscale a rough photo into a clean headshot while preserving the person's identity.</Tip>

## Data source

- **Route page:** `apps/houseloop-crm/app/(crm)/settings/profile/page.tsx` ‚Äî client component, `GET /api/profile` ‚Üí redirect.
- **`/api/profile`** (`GET` / `PATCH`) ‚Äî `apps/houseloop-crm/app/api/profile/route.ts`. Reads/writes the current user's row in **`hl_users`**: `id`, `full_name`, `email`, `role`, `phone`, `timezone`, `avatar_url`, and `data->>'notification_email'`. `PATCH` updates `full_name`, `phone`, `timezone`, and `data.notification_email`, then re-issues the session cookie. (The redirect page itself only calls `GET`; edits made in the member editor go through `PATCH /api/team/users/{id}`.)
- **`/api/team/users/[id]`** ‚Äî the member editor's primary load/save. Joins **`hl_users`** with role assignments (**`hl_member_role`**) and connection state: **`hl_oauth_connections`**, **`hl_dialer_assignments`**, **`hl_imessage_assignments`**, **`hl_calendar_connections`**. Editor at `apps/houseloop-crm/app/(crm)/settings/team/agents/[id]/page.tsx`.
- **`/api/team/roles`** ‚Äî the role catalog (human and AI-kind roles).
- **`/api/me`** ‚Äî the viewer's identity + access level, used to gate the Access dropdown.
- **`/api/team/users/[id]/roles/[roleId]`** (`PATCH`) ‚Äî persists per-role overrides (persona/tools/docs/workflow_ids) on **`hl_member_role`**.

**Contact Cards:**

- **`/api/team/vcard/[id]`** (`GET`, public) ‚Äî serves the savable `.vcf` (`text/vcard`), headshot embedded as base64. Built by `apps/houseloop-crm/lib/vcard.ts` (`buildVcard` / `vcardForUser`).
- **`/api/team/card-image/[id]`** (`GET`, public) ‚Äî the Digital Business Card PNG (Satori / `next/og`), `apps/houseloop-crm/app/api/team/card-image/[id]/route.tsx`.
- **`/api/team/card/[id]`** (`GET`) ‚Äî the card's display fields as JSON (drives the in-editor previews).
- **`/api/team/cards`** (`GET`) ‚Äî the card-able roster (humans + Myli; clones excluded) for the composer picker.
- **`/c/[id]`** ‚Äî the public web contact page (headshot + "Save to Contacts"). Renderers: `components/team/ContactCardPreview.tsx` and `components/team/VcardSentPreview.tsx`.
- The editable card fields are stored on `hl_users.data`: `title`, `card_phone`, `card_email`, `card_note`, `card_homepage`. `PATCH /api/team/users/{id}` accepts each. All three public routes are allowlisted in `middleware.ts`.

No Inngest functions are involved; all writes are synchronous CRM API calls.

## How it fits

Profile Settings is the User Settings entry point into HouseLoop's single member model: every teammate ‚Äî human or AI agent ‚Äî is one row edited through one page, and "your profile" is just your own row in it. Because your profile is your own member record, the same editor governs access levels, comms connections, and AI-clone configuration, keeping signing-in identity (name/phone/time zone, Google-managed email), permissions, and channels in one place rather than a separate account screen.
