**In the app:** CRM sidebar → **User Settings** → **Business Settings** — [`/settings/business-profile`](https://crm.houseloop.ai/settings/business-profile)

## What it does

Business Settings is the single source of truth for your company's identity. You set it once here and the rest of the CRM reads from it so nothing drifts:

- The **business timezone** you pick drives how every date and time renders across the CRM.
- Your **company name and address** feed the CAN-SPAM compliance footer on bulk emails.
- The **business-hours window** (configured inline on this page) lets automation delay steps hold their actions until the next moment inside your working hours.

The page renders a static brand logo (from the brand-asset source of truth); logo upload and custom palette are noted in-app as "coming soon" and are not part of this version.

## What you configure

The page has two save-able sections. The top **Business Profile** form saves independently from the nested **Business Hours** section — each has its own Save button.

### Business Profile

| Field | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Business Name | Company name; used in the email compliance footer |
| Business Timezone | IANA timezone the whole CRM renders dates/times in (default `America/Chicago` / Central) |
| Street Address, City, State, ZIP, County | Physical company address; feeds the email footer |
| Sender Name | Default outbound sender display name |
| Sender Email | Default outbound sender email |

Timezone choices offered: Central, Eastern, Mountain, Arizona (no DST), Pacific, Alaska, and Hawaii.

### Business Hours (nested inline)

| Field | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Enable business hours | Master toggle for the window |
| Start Time / End Time | The daily open/close window (`HH:MM`) |
| Timezone | IANA timezone the window is evaluated in |
| Skip weekends | When on, actions falling on Sat/Sun roll to the next weekday's open |

When enabled, automations with "Execute only during business hours" checked delay their actions to the next moment inside this window. Individual automations can override these defaults with their own schedule.

## ⏰ Timezone — the ONE law (why an 8 PM appointment kept showing as 9 PM)

<Warning>
**HouseLoop is a Kansas City business → everything renders in CENTRAL (`America/Chicago`). Never
`America/New_York` — that is EASTERN and is one hour ahead.** A recurring bug (hit ~50×, fixed 2026-07-22)
had appointments and lead-detail times display **one hour late** (an 8:00 PM Central appointment read
9:00 PM). The stored data was always correct (`8 PM CDT = 01:00 UTC`); the render was wrong.
</Warning>

**Root cause:** display formatters **hardcoded** `timeZone: 'America/New_York'` — including a function
literally named `fmtCentral()` that rendered *Eastern* — and `lib/business-profile.ts` defaulted to
`America/New_York` while a comment called it "KC/Central." The lie kept regenerating the bug every time a new
time-rendering component was written by copy-paste.

### The law for developers (never hardcode a zone)

There is exactly ONE place timezones are defined — **`apps/houseloop-crm/lib/datetime.ts`** — and every
date/time render MUST go through it. Never write a `timeZone:` literal in a component.

| Surface | Use | Resolves to |
|---|---|---|
| Any **business-wide** time (calendar, settings, dashboards, notifications, server-side merge tokens) | `BUSINESS_TZ` / `fmtBusinessTime()` / `fmtBusinessDateTime()` | `America/Chicago` (Central) — or the value in Business Settings |
| Any time **shown on a lead** (lead detail, appointments, activity feed, streams) | `leadDisplayTz()` | the **lead's** zone (from their phone area code, mig 0375), falling back to Central |

- **Lead-detail law (Zach 2026-07-10):** times *on a lead* render in the **lead's** timezone, set once per
  render by `CenterLane` (`setLeadDisplayTz`) and read by every lead-scoped formatter via `leadDisplayTz()`.
  A 913 (Kansas City) lead is Central, so it reads Central — the same as the business. Every lead-detail
  component (Next Actions, Appointments, Emails/AI/Deals streams) reads `leadDisplayTz()` so they can never
  disagree with `CenterLane` again.
- **The store is always UTC.** `hl_appointments.starts_at` / `hl_tasks.due_at` are UTC instants. Input
  wall-clock → UTC via `wallTimeToUtcISO()`; UTC → a `<input type="datetime-local">` via `utcToWallInput()`.
  Both are DST-aware (they compute the real Central offset for that date — `−5` CDT in summer, `−6` CST in winter).
- **INPUT side — never `new Date()` a picked wall-time (Zach 2026-07-23, the recurrence).** When a form turns a
  picked date + time into a stored timestamp, it MUST interpret that wall-clock as **Central**, not the editor's
  browser zone. **Banned:** `` new Date(`${date}T${time}`).toISOString() `` — `new Date` on a zone-less string reads
  the *browser's* timezone, so an Eastern browser stores "8:00 AM" as 8am **Eastern**, which then renders 7:00 AM
  Central. **Correct:** `` wallTimeToUtcISO(`${date}T${time}:00`) ``. This is exactly how a Task set for 8am kept
  showing 7am (`TaskComposerModal` used `new Date`; the appointment modal already used `wallTimeToUtcISO`). The
  two must match. ⚠️ The `hl/no-raw-timezone` linter catches the *display* side but NOT this input pattern — grep
  for `` new Date(`...T...`).toISOString() `` when auditing a new date-picker form.
- **The business zone is configurable** in Business Settings above (`getBusinessTimezone()` reads the DB), and
  `BUSINESS_TZ` is the default/fallback. `business_hours.timezone` must match (it was wrongly Eastern; fixed
  2026-07-22).

**If you catch yourself typing `timeZone: 'America/New_York'` (or any zone literal) in a formatter — STOP.**
Import `BUSINESS_TZ`/`leadDisplayTz` from `lib/datetime` instead.

### Enforced by the linter (so it can't come back)

This is not a guideline you have to remember — the ESLint rule **`hl/no-raw-timezone`**
(`apps/houseloop-crm/eslint-rules/no-raw-timezone.js`, `'error'`) fails the build on **both** bug classes:

1. a hardcoded `timeZone: '<zone>'` literal (any `America/*` — use the `BUSINESS_TZ` constant), and
2. a `toLocaleString`/`toLocaleTimeString` that renders a **time** (`hour`) with **no** `timeZone` (browser-local).

The only allowed literal is `timeZone: 'UTC'`, reserved for **date-only** renders that must never shift zones
(closing dates, milestones). A full-tree sweep took the CRM to **zero** violations (2026-07-22), and the rule
holds it there — a new hardcoded or browser-local time render can't merge. That is why this bug is closed for good.

## Data source

Both sections persist as singleton rows in the `hl_settings` table (CRM database), stored as JSON:

- **Business Profile** → `hl_settings` key `business_profile` — read via `getBusinessProfile()` (`lib/business-profile.ts`), written via `PUT /api/settings/business-profile` (an upsert that merges partial saves over the current value).
- **Business Hours** → `hl_settings` key `business_hours` — read via `getBusinessHours()` (`lib/business-hours.ts`), written via `PUT /api/settings/business-hours`. `GET /api/settings/business-profile` returns the current profile.

The page loads both values server-side and passes them to the client forms. If `hl_settings` is unreachable, reads fall back to defaults (timezone `America/Chicago`; business hours treated as not configured / passthrough).

## How it fits

Business Settings is a foundational settings page other CRM features depend on rather than a standalone tool:

- **Date/time rendering** across the CRM pins to the `timezone` set here (via `getBusinessTimezone()`).
- **Bulk email compliance footer** pulls `company_name` and the address fields.
- **Automation delay steps** call `clampToBusinessHours()` (`lib/business-hours.ts`) to shift a scheduled action to the next in-window moment when business hours are enabled.

Business Hours previously had its own standalone menu item; it now lives inline within Business Settings as a section, because it is a business-profile setting rather than a separate pipeline step.
